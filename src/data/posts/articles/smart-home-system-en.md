# 🏠 My Mom Asked Me Where the Kitchen Light Switch Is

## Me: Switch? We Don't Have Switches Here, We Have Voice

— How to Plan Your Smart Home During Renovation Without Screwing Up

---

A lot of people want to go all-in on smart home tech during renovation. The result is usually:

* Half the lights work by voice, half are manual only
* AC is on App A, curtains on App B
* When grandma visits, the whole system breaks down
* Eventually, everything goes back to physical switches

Let's be clear:

> ❌ Smart home isn't bad
> ❌ You just didn't "think in systems" from the start

---

# 🧠 1. The Biggest Smart Home Trap: You Think You're "Upgrading Devices," But You're Actually "Designing a System"

The common mistake people make during renovation:

* Buy a smart switch when they see one
* Install a smart light when they see one
* Add any cool device they find

The result:

> A pile of "smart gadgets" with no "unified brain"

Real smart home isn't about stacking devices. It's a three-layer structure:

---

## 🧩 1. The Hub (Brain)

👉 The system that controls everything

For example:

* Alibaba Tmall Genie
* Xiaomi Mi Home
* Huawei HarmonyOS Smart Home
* Home Assistant (advanced)

📌 Core principle:

> One home, one master system. No parallel systems.

---

## 💡 2. Execution Layer (Devices)

Includes:

* Lights
* AC
* Curtains
* Door locks
* Ventilation / underfloor heating
* TV / speakers

📌 Key principle:

> All devices must connect to the same system

Otherwise they all do their own thing.

---

## 📡 3. Connection Layer (Protocol)

90% of people overlook this:

* Zigbee (recommended)
* Wi-Fi (prone to congestion)
* Bluetooth (okay for small devices)
* Matter (the future)

📌 In one sentence:

> No unified protocol = your smart home will crash

---

# ⚠️ 2. The 5 Deadliest Renovation Mistakes (Almost Everyone Makes Them)

## ❌ Mistake 1: Buy Devices First, Think About the System Later

Result:

* Device A supports Mi Home
* Device B only supports its own app
* Device C is Bluetooth-only

👉 End result: 10 apps on your phone's home screen

---

## ❌ Mistake 2: Use Wi-Fi for Everything

Seems convenient, but causes the biggest problems:

* Too many devices = disconnects
* Router overload
* Bad latency

👉 Correct approach:

> Wi-Fi only for "heavy devices." Light devices use Zigbee.

---

## ❌ Mistake 3: Ignore the Neutral Wire

Many smart switches require:

* Neutral wire + live wire

Skip it during renovation → total failure later

👉 One of the most common do-overs in smart home renovation

---

## ❌ Mistake 4: Smart ≠ Voice-Only

Many people think:

> "Installed a smart speaker = smart home"

Reality:

* Voice is just an input method
* Automation is what makes it truly smart

---

## ❌ Mistake 5: No Scene Design

It's not about "buying devices." It's about:

* Coming home scene
* Sleeping scene
* Night bathroom scene
* Cooking scene

No scenes = never truly smart

---

# 🏗️ 3. The Correct Renovation Sequence (Follow This and You Won't Regret It)

## ✔️ Step 1: Choose Your System First (Most Important)

Pick one of three:

* Xiaomi Mi Home (best value)
* Apple HomeKit (stable ecosystem)
* Huawei (integrated ecosystem)

📌 Principle:

> Choose the brain first, then the organs

---

## ✔️ Step 2: Plan Your Wiring (Determines Success or Failure)

Must plan ahead:

* Neutral wire or not?
* Pre-lay Ethernet cables?
* Zigbee gateway location
* Router Mesh layout

📌 Advice:

> If you can run a wire, don't rely entirely on wireless

---

## ✔️ Step 3: Only Choose Devices That Connect to One System

Prioritize:

* Zigbee devices
* Same-ecosystem devices (same brand)
* Matter-compatible devices (future-proof)

---

## ✔️ Step 4: Design 3 Must-Have Automation Scenes

Minimum recommended setup:

### 🌙 Night Bathroom Mode

* Dim lights turn on automatically
* Hallway motion sensor lights activate

### 🏠 Coming Home Mode

* Door unlocks → lights on → AC starts

### 😴 Sleep Mode

* One-button lights off
* AC adjusts temperature
* Curtains close

---

# 🧩 4. What Real "Advanced Smart Home" Looks Like

Not:

> "Hey Siri, turn on the lights"

But:

The moment you walk through the door:

* Lights turn on automatically (no command needed)
* AC is already at your preferred temperature
* Curtains close by themselves
* Music switches to a gentle playlist
* Entryway light is at 30% brightness

👉 You don't even need to "operate" anything

---

# 🧓 5. The Most Realistic Scenario (Most People Will Experience This)

You think you're building:

> A fully smart home

Six months later:

* Living room lights: smart
* Bedroom lights: manual (because Wi-Fi keeps dropping)
* Parents' room: old-school switch (too complicated otherwise)
* Kitchen: voice + manual hybrid

👉 The universal conclusion:

> "Physical switches are still the most reliable"

---

# 🧠 6. The One Sentence That Actually Prevents Mistakes

> Smart home isn't a "device upgrade." It's a "system design."

---

# 🏁 Final Summary (Very Important)

If you only remember three things:

### ① System first, devices second

### ② Wiring first, shopping second

### ③ Scenes first, features second
