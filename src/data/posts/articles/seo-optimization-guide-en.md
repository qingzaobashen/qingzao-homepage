# SEO: 10 Practical Rules to Avoid Being Eliminated by Search Engines

You've heard too much vague advice like "just write good content and rankings will follow."

This article skips the platitudes and covers only actionable, battle-tested SEO methods that still work in 2026.

---

## 1. Keyword Strategy: Don't Write What People Search, Answer What They Ask

### Wrong Approach

Find the keyword "renovation budget," write an article titled "Renovation Budget," and repeat the phrase everywhere.

Google knows you're keyword-stuffing and won't reward you.

### Right Approach

When someone searches "renovation budget," they really want to know:

> "My apartment is 90sqm — how much will it actually cost? Where does the money go? How do I avoid going over budget?"

So your title should answer that question directly.

### How to Do It

- Use Google Search Console to find queries that already surface your site
- Use Ahrefs or Semrush for related keyword analysis
- Look at the "People also ask" section in search results

---

## 2. Content Structure: Make Your Article Instantly Scannable

Google's crawler doesn't "read" articles — it reads structure. Clear structure helps Google understand your core content.

### Heading Hierarchy

```
# Main Title (H1, unique per page)
## Key Point (H2)
### Detailed Explanation (H3)
#### Supplementary or Examples (H4)
```

### Paragraph Length

3-4 lines on mobile, no more than 4-5 lines on desktop. Long paragraphs get skipped.

### When to Use Lists

**Use lists for**: parallel items, steps, comparisons
**Use paragraphs for**: narrative, explanation, analysis

---

## 3. Title Tags: The Only Thing You Need to Optimize Precisely

Many people obsess over Meta Description, but Google no longer uses it as a ranking factor.

The Title Tag remains the **most important on-page SEO element**.

### Good Title Formula

**Core keyword + Differentiator + Emotional trigger**

❌ "Renovation Budget Guide"
✅ "2026 Renovation Budget: How Much Does a 90sqm Home Really Cost? (With Detailed Tables)"

### Length

Keep it under 60 characters. Mobile truncation is even shorter, so put keywords early.

---

## 4. Site Speed: 1 Second Costs You 20% Conversion

Core Web Vitals aren't optional — they're mandatory.

### Real Data

- 1s → 3s load time: bounce rate increases 32%
- 1s → 5s load time: bounce rate increases 90%
- 1s → 6s load time: bounce rate increases 106%

### Optimization Priority

1. **Image compression**: Use WebP (30% smaller than JPG), resize to no more than 2x display size
2. **Remove unnecessary JavaScript**: Every third-party script slows you down
3. **Use a CDN**: Cloudflare's free plan provides basic acceleration
4. **Enable browser caching**: Set 7-30 day cache for static assets

---

## 5. Mobile: It's Not "Adaptation," It's "Priority"

Google uses Mobile-First Indexing. If your mobile experience is poor, desktop rankings suffer too.

### Practical Steps

- Test every page in Chrome DevTools mobile mode
- Ensure buttons and links are at least 48px tall with adequate spacing
- Set font size to at least 16px
- Never let content exceed screen width (horizontal scrolling is the #1 mobile UX disaster)

---

## 6. Backlinks: Quality Over Quantity

One link from an authoritative site is worth 100 links from spam sites.

### The Right Way to Get Links

- **Resource-based**: Write a high-quality guide that people naturally cite
- **Guest blogging**: Write for other sites in your industry, include a link in your bio
- **Broken link building**: Find dead links on relevant sites, suggest your content as a replacement

### Never Do These

- Buy backlinks
- Join link exchange programs
- Spam comment sections or forum signatures

Google's Penguin algorithm specifically targets these tactics. Getting caught means ranking drops or deindexing.

---

## 7. User Experience: Google Increasingly Thinks Like a User

Google's ultimate goal: users find the answer on your page and don't hit the back button.

### Signals

- **Dwell time**: How long users stay on your page
- **Bounce rate**: Percentage of users who leave immediately
- **Pogo-sticking**: User clicks your result → leaves immediately → clicks another result

### Improvements

- Answer the question in the first paragraph
- Use subheadings for scannability
- Short paragraphs, lists, bold key sentences

---

## 8. Technical SEO: Common Beginner Mistakes

### Checklist

- [ ] Every page has a unique Title Tag
- [ ] Every page has a unique H1
- [ ] 404 pages return a 404 status code (not 200)
- [ ] sitemap.xml exists and is submitted to Google Search Console
- [ ] robots.txt exists and doesn't block important pages
- [ ] No duplicate content (same content on multiple URLs)
- [ ] Clean URL structure containing keywords

---

## 9. Content Updates: Old Articles Are More Valuable Than New Ones

Many think SEO means constantly publishing new articles. In reality, **updating old articles often yields the highest ROI**.

### Approach

- Review your top 20 traffic-generating articles quarterly
- Update outdated data, add new insights
- Improve titles and meta descriptions
- Add internal links to newer relevant articles

An article ranked #15 can jump to top 3 after a thorough update.

---

## 10. Be Patient

SEO's cruelest reality: you'll see almost no results for the first 3-6 months (unless your site already has high authority).

Most people quit. Those who persist get to the top.

Measure quarterly traffic trends, not weekly ranking fluctuations.

---

## Conclusion

SEO's technical barriers keep lowering as Google gets smarter. But competition keeps rising — because everyone knows a bit of SEO now.

The ultimate differentiator remains: **Is your content genuinely useful?**

If yes, SEO techniques simply help deliver that value to the people who need it faster.
