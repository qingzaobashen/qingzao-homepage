## 6 Spaces, 100+ Design Details from Those Who've Been There

### 1. Kitchen
"The golden kitchen, silver bathroom" — the kitchen is where money goes but effect shows fastest:
1. **Dishwasher (built-in)**: if only one happiness-boosting appliance, a 13+ place-setting large-capacity dishwasher. It mainly solves marital harmony.
2. **Multi-height countertops**: sink/prep raised (~85–90cm), cooktop lowered (~75–80cm). This 5–10cm drop saves your back and shoulders — ergonomics at its best.
3. **Kitchen circulation**: wash → cut → cook → serve is most rational, unless you live in a mansion.
4. **Large single sink**: easy for pots; a movable small basin comes with the large one if needed.
5. **Undermount (or flush) sink**: no mold, easy care; counter residue pushes straight in. (Reinforce undermount with steel brackets.)
6. **Cooking robot**: my wife praises it daily — keeps oil off her, less fume, no need to learn cooking.
7. **Outlet with switch**: countertop appliances stay plugged in, no unplugging.
8. **Wall cabinets to ceiling**: must reach ceiling — no fume or dust.
9. **Range hood backflow preventer**: must install; rented places smelled of neighbors' cooking at every meal, greasy kitchen over time.
10. **Quartz countertop**: stain/scrape resistant, cost-effective.
11. **Built-in flat-embedded fridge**: no dust on top, storage above, also aesthetic.
12. **Cross-door / French four-door fridge**: drawers below, sorted, easy pull-out.
13. **Light strip under wall cabinets**: clear cutting, essential for the clumsy.
14. **No backsplash strip**: it only hides ugliness; square and level the kitchen wall so you can skip it — one less hard-to-clean corner.
15. **Plan sockets & water inlet under sink**: dishwasher, disposer, purifier — many things go there.
16. **Garbage disposer**: a real gem — no hand-digging sink waste, high happiness.
17. **Large wall tiles in middle**: grander, fewer seams, easier care; small tiles = more seams, hard to clean.
18. **Wall-track sliding door**: no hygiene dead corner, but sways; self-adhesive floor-track slides don't sway but have dead corners, annoying to clean.
19. **AC in kitchen**: too hot in summer to enter; an AC would be nice.
20. **All drawers**: ditch the door-front base cabinets. Deep drawers + dividers raise storage ~30% and save squatting to reach the back.
21. **Corner staggered levels**: blocked by the dishwasher position, couldn't do corner staggered levels — fetching corner pots is annoying now.
22. **More countertop**: though pricey, more is great; now I prep on the dining table, small appliances overflow — add more when possible.
23. **No gap between hood and wall cabinet**: fume gets in, wipe daily. Make the hood hook adjustable/raisable so it sits up during install.
24. **Bad water-inlet planning**: the master put a main valve where my dishwasher should go, unnoticed at acceptance — dishwasher couldn't sit by the sink.
25. **Stainless steel countertop**: unaffordable — 5mm thick still thousands per meter; skip if budget-tight.
26. **Enamel board**: haven't felt its benefit; wall looks cluttered with too much on it; may differ from most.

### 2. Bathroom
Core: easy care, no mold, minimalist.
1. **Wall-drain hand basin**: hide the drain in the wall; no floor dead corner, robot vacuum roams free.
2. **Smart toilet**: semi-smart is enough, but no auto-open; full-smart is said to flush when you pass by.
3. **Ceramic integrated basin**: vs. slab-spliced (leaks, dead corners), the ceramic one's smooth arcs are a clean freak's delight.
4. **Waterproofing past the door**: only then fully avoid seepage into the wall; also a waterproof dam at the threshold.
5. **Soundproof-wrapped pipes**: wrap drainpipes, especially horizontal — else night noise drives you mad.
6. **Bathroom slope**: watch it, or pooling yellows easily.
7. **Dry/wet separation**: do it; at least a shower curtain if not.
8. **Spray gun**: easy to rinse floor/walls.
9. **Mirror cabinet**: very storage-friendly, recommended.
10. **Drawer vanity**: door-front cabinets make you squat; drawers are easier.
11. **Hidden floor drain**: tiny flow, but fits dry zones (toilet, vanity).
12. **Small kitchen boiler**: my master bath is far from the heater, hot water takes forever; a small boiler gives quick hot water — but reserve a power point.
13. **Thermostatic shower**: no temperature swings (especially when kitchen uses water). The thermostatic cartridge's stable feel is low-cost, high-return.
14. **Toilet outlet splash-proof**: outlets the shower may hit need covers; mine sit against the doorframe, no cover possible — too many electrical details to plan fully at design.
15. **Defog mirror**: only a small patch defogs — almost funny; just wipe by hand.
16. **Bathtub**: personal; do it if you bathe; skip if unsure.
17. **Silicone**: any price yellows/molds; resign yourself, scrape and re-caulk when unbearable.
18. **Epoxy grout**: bathroom recommend epoxy sanded grout — extra waterproofing, but not white (stains hard to clean).

### 3. Living & dining room
Defines the home's look and the owner's taste.
1. **Linear sensor light strip**: sideboard, corridor. Light on arrival — safe and premium.
2. **"Redundant" outlet layout**:
  * Under/beside dining table: hot pot or laptop power, or just an island.
  * Beside sofa: USB outlets.
3. **Double-layer hidden light strip**: collects dust but looks great, raises ceiling, dust unseen.
4. **Few open shelves**: fewer is better; annoying to dust.
5. **50mm tube behind TV**: cables through the wall, clean.
6. **TV cabinet without drawers**: use a movable top panel to access cables/power.
7. **No coffee table**: small/mid layouts — use a cart or side table instead, more space.
8. **Buy a big TV**: clarity is high now, watch close; 3m→100in, 85in is mainstream.
9. **Ceiling-height sideboard**: can be finished or bought; ceiling-height wins by no-top dusting.
10. **Toast sofa**: adjustable seat width — youth lounge wide, elders narrow for lumbar support.
11. **Full-wall TV cabinet**: stores clutter, very practical.
12. **Monitor on separate circuit**: independent power for long absences.
13. **No main light**: lowers ceiling; if low, use ceiling light. Done well it's pretty though.
14. **Return-shaped ceiling**: collects dust, feels heavy; but needed if running pipes.
15. **Rug**: photogenic, nice underfoot, but harbors dirt.
16. **Sofa-side outlet position**: plan sofa placement first, then outlet; don't let the sofa block it.
17. **Projector**: poor quality, unwatchable by day; just get a TV.

### 4. Bedroom
Sleep and clothes habits decide the design.
1. **Curtain box + track**: blackout ≥90%, sleep till natural wake; no Roman rod (morning light through gaps hurts sleep).
2. **Head not by window**: head cold → headaches.
3. **More hanging space**: more is better; wash-and-hang, no folding, no wrinkles.
4. **Foot-of-bed wardrobe**: if possible, prettier; mind size — depth 60cm, aisle ≥50cm.
5. **Metal wardrobe**: flexible layout, no formaldehyde — should be great, but couldn't find a vendor then.
6. **Light strip in wardrobe**: bedroom light never bright enough when finding clothes.
7. **No transparent wardrobe**: only for show homes; becomes a mess later.
8. **Good mattress**: huge sleep impact; ~3000 RMB budget enough; beyond that, diminishing returns.

### 5. Entryway
1. **Smart lock**: face recognition, no hands, camera — max security.
2. **Two open layers in shoe cabinet**: one for slippers, one for outdoor shoes.
3. **Shoe-changing bench + hanging zone**: two drawers below, open hanging above (bags, shoe-changing).
4. **Outlet in shoe cabinet**: winter shoe dryer; warm shoes on leaving.
5. **Entryway outlet**: hardly needed anything — never used.
6. **Entryway touch light strip**: touch-sensor, opened a few times a year — should've been sensor.
7. **Ultra-thin shoe cabinet**: tiny capacity, only small shoes; basketball shoes won't fit.

### 6. Balcony
1. **Dryer**: absolute necessity in the south, especially where "returning humidity" hits; pair with wool balls.
2. **Multiple washers**: my wife bought 5 — one for shoes/socks, two for underwear, one for outerwear, one for kids' clothes — satisfied daily.
3. **800-deep utility cabinet**: if possible, plan one. Super-deep cabinet = small storage room; no bottom board, height >1.3m for floor items.
4. **Hidden drying rod**: mature now, both light and rod; thought fragile, many online complaints, so skipped.
5. **Hanging chair, treadmill, etc.**: per habit; without the habit, likely becomes a "half-dirty clothes" zone.

### 7. Whole-house details
1. **Whole-house soundproofing**: drainpipes wrapped, bedroom silent magnetic locks. Quiet is the most expensive attribute of quality space.
2. **Robot vacuum**: reserve water in/out and outlet in balcony or cabinet for auto drain/refill; only change the dust bag every few months, never touched a broom since.
3. **Multi-function long table**: next home, I'd replace the "sofa vs TV" layout with one long table — dining, office, craft, the family's communication center.
4. **Continuous tile/floor laying**: whole-house tiles/flooring continuous, grander.
5. **Whole-house smart**: smart switches, appliances, locks, curtains — voice control, "speak not touch."
6. **Double half-dirty-clothes zones**: one in living/balcony for arriving, one in bedroom for changing to home clothes.
7. **Handleless cabinet doors**: embedded or 45° bevel handles — unified look, prevents bumps.
8. **Rounded corners**: whole-house external corners rounded — cabinets, wall corners — prevent kids' bumps.

> **Pitfall lesson**: in renovation, **"reducing hygiene dead corners"** matters far more than **"adding fancy shapes."** Any design requiring you to bend down to wipe or climb to reach will become a dusty regret.
