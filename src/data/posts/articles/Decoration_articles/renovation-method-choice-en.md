## How to Choose Among 5 Renovation Models — The Most Important Decision Before You Start

Renovation should be a joyful event — building your dream home. But in reality, many people, having chosen the wrong renovation company, slide from excitement into endless trouble. So take weeks or even months to investigate, compare, and weigh your options carefully. This choice largely determines your mood and cost over the coming months.

### 1. Types of renovation models
![Renovation model overview](/images/articles/renovation-method-choice/1-装修方式概览.jpg)
![Renovation company vs design studio](/images/articles/renovation-method-choice/3-装修公司与设计工作室.jpg)
![Designer vs foreman](/images/articles/renovation-method-choice/4-设计师与工长.jpg)
By contract scope, the market offers: renovation company, design studio, independent designer, independent foreman, and self-managed renovation.

1. **Renovation company**: delegate part or all of the work. Divided into half-package, full-package, and all-in-one package; generally suits owners with limited time and energy.
   - **Half-package**: you source main materials (tiles, latex paint) and coordinate delivery and scheduling; design, construction, and auxiliary materials come from the company. The core is "control over main materials" while leveraging the company's expertise.
   - **Full-package**: construction plus all main and auxiliary materials — you buy nothing.
   - **All-in-one package**: on top of full-package, the company also buys major furniture and some soft furnishings, so you can move in directly.
   - Note: design studios below can also do half/full/all-in-one; independent designers vary — ask.
2. **Design studio**: between a renovation company and an independent designer; small, run by a few designers, lower price, flexible personalization, but you must control material quality yourself and depend heavily on the studio's ability and reputation.
3. **Independent designer**: a solo professional, usually offering end-to-end service from design to construction — including design, foreman referral, and material guidance. Higher design fee; the service model resembles a company's all-in-one package.
4. **Independent foreman**: a solo professional responsible for construction execution only — no design or material sourcing. The model resembles a company's half-package but at a lower price.
5. **Self-managed renovation**: you buy all materials, hire workers yourself, and handle design and supervision. Suits experienced owners with plenty of time.

### 2. Choose by your situation
![Renovation model decision](/images/articles/renovation-method-choice/2-装修方式决策.jpg)
- **Time**: limited time/energy → company full-package or independent designer (comprehensive management, less involvement). But watch contract terms to avoid poor quality or unreasonable extra charges.
- **Budget**: tight control → company half-package, independent foreman, or self-managed (clear upfront construction cost, you control material spend).
- **Result**: high expectations on look → design studio, company all-in-one, or independent designer (detailed plans, visual renderings, contractual guarantee of execution).
- **Experience**: experienced and time-rich → self-managed, full participation and control, personalized design and management, and lower overall cost.

### 3. Characteristics of each model
#### 3.1 Renovation company
![Choosing a renovation company](/images/articles/renovation-method-choice/company-choice.jpg)
1. **Doesn't employ workers**: an open secret — many companies don't keep workers; after signing, they assign the job to an independent foreman who hires the crew. So company vs. foreman may mean the same people, but the foreman costs 15–20% less. The company offers supervision and after-sales, more reassuring.
2. **Partnerships with material merchants**: companies collaborate with material shops; some are essentially showrooms profiting mainly from materials, with installation also done by those merchants. They thus dislike half-package (no material margin) and charge more for it. But bulk buying can make them cheaper than you shopping alone — compare early.
3. **Randomly matched foreman & designer**: even good companies assign specific people; the key is that the matched designer and foreman are reliable, especially the foreman — partly luck. Request a steady, dependable foreman and meet them first. If unsatisfied later, use the company's system to replace them.
4. **Online vs. offline strategy**: some target offline (middle-aged/elderly, discounts, ground promotion near new developments); some target online (younger, high ratings, review management). Know which you're dealing with.
5. **On extra charges**: changes are inevitable and reasonable, but the company must disclose its product traits and likely extra-charge areas upfront. Hiding them is a scam to profit from add-ons.
6. **Differentiation**: companies have styles and competing strengths (hard quality, design execution, speed, eco-friendliness) — pick by your needs.
7. **Regional differences**: southern companies emphasize moisture-proofing and ventilation; northern ones emphasize warmth. A northern-blooded company in the south may bring mismatched habits.
8. **Packages**: various packages lack flexibility — choosing one may not meet personal needs (packages are the basis for bulk material deals).
9. **Warm early, cold later**: companies are very attentive upfront because they collect a high upfront payment that locks you in.
10. **Beware of runaway risk**: years ago many companies took large upfront fees then vanished mid-project. Non-runaway is the baseline — avoid small companies or excessive upfront payment.
11. **Beware of low-price risk**: very low packages often omit required items (betting you don't know) then charge extra, or use 20-year-old techniques and poor materials requiring paid upgrades. Classic profit tactic.

#### 3.2 Design studio
![Choosing a design studio](/images/articles/renovation-method-choice/studio-choice.jpg)
1. **Designer-led**: usually a few experienced, capable designers who left big companies, with some crew/material connections for lower prices.
2. **Deep personalization**: flexible, no package limits, but this often causes extra charges and possibly messy management; unreliable studios may quote randomly.
3. **Small and slow**: fewer staff means weaker intake and limited crews — longer schedules.
4. **Pursuit of execution**: chases "small-but-beautiful" results, sometimes with gimmicky designs you should weigh. May push material/craft upgrades, making budget uncontrollable.
5. **Easy to fold**: small scale, concentrated in tier-1/2 cities, vulnerable to market shocks. Check founding age and strength to avoid无人负责 later.

#### 3.3 Independent designer
1. Workflow similar to a studio, just solo; some do design only, some partner with crews/material merchants — hiring one can equal a full-package.
2. **Generally solid design skill**: those who go solo are usually capable.
3. **Pursuit of execution**: like studios, builds reputation through realized cases.
4. **Design fees generally high**: one-on-one service solving most problems, but verify real skill — some steal others' work for promotion.
5. **High build cost**: high design fee + worry-free usually means high construction cost; designers pick clients they can satisfy.
6. **Companion style**: some newcomers offer "companion-style" renovation, befriending owners and solving everything.
7. **Kickbacks**: designers often partner with material merchants, so letting them buy everything risks kickbacks.

#### 3.4 Independent foreman (crew)
1. **Foreman = the crew**: he calls the shots; many are ex-company foremen taking private jobs or veterans. They get work by referral (old clients, designers) — word of mouth.
2. **Lower price**: no company cut, more affordable — good for saving budget.
3. **Decent craft**: work is actually fine; many rely on reputation, some trained at正规 companies. It depends on whether he cares.
4. **Hard to find a reliable one**: quality depends on the foreman's attentiveness; self-found quality is uncontrollable, and relatives/friends are not recommended — pitfalls abound yet you can't complain due to the relationship.
5. **No after-sales**: basically none; some even disappear.
6. **Beware of runaway**: long projects need character assessment; if he runs, you lose big. Crews are quite free.

#### 3.5 Self-managed renovation
1. You buy materials, hire workers, and handle design and supervision — suits capable, experienced, time-rich people, or those in the trade.
2. **Saves money**: bypass company overhead and profit; every yuan's destination is clear, less "being played."
3. **You're in charge**: you're the "chief designer" — from style to every tile color, by your rules, a unique home. Freedom and achievement hard to get from a company.
4. **Tests your eye**: all materials sourced and picked by you — brand, eco grade — reassuring. But if unfamiliar with the market, you may overpay or be fooled with fakes, losing both money and quality.
5. **Unprofessional design**: laypeople relying on ideas or patched online inspiration easily plan poorly — awkward circulation, insufficient storage — only noticed after moving in, costly to fix.
6. **Tedious worker coordination**: find each trade yourself; their skill decides quality, so you watch daily. Sequencing (plumbing→tiling→carpentry) is on you; poor handoff causes blame-shifting — exhausting.
7. **Extremely time/energy consuming**: from start to finish, everything is on you — market, pricing, site — feels like losing a layer of skin.
8. **No later guarantee**: scattered laborers vanish after work; if leaks appear, hard to find anyone responsible; repair and rights protection are a hassle.

Choosing a renovation model concerns the cost, result, and peace of mind of the coming months. Whether you prefer worry-free or ultimate personalization, the key is knowing your needs and weighing each model's traits. May this guide clear the fog and help you decide wisely, making your home-building journey smoother and more reassuring.
