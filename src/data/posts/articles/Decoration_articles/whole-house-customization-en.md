# Whole-house Customization Knowledge

Whole-house customization means customizing storage cabinets based on the shape of your home space to meet your storage needs while keeping the home tidy and beautiful, e.g. floor-to-ceiling cabinets avoid the trouble of cleaning the top.

---

## 1. Whole-house Customization Choices

There are mainly three ways for whole-house customization:
- **Brand custom merchant**: Such as "Oppein", "Sogal" and other custom brands, like "full package" in hard renovation — worry-free but expensive; but in recent years, brand customization reputation is getting worse; if you must choose, Sogal should be OK (heard from an installer).
- **Custom factory**: Find a factory specialized in customization, let them design and process directly; saves the price difference earned by the brand.
- **On-site carpentry**: Like "clear package" in hard renovation, flexible but depends on worker skill.

Decide based on your needs:
1. **Be clear about what you want**: function, storage, style priority; with enough budget, brand customization offers more style choices.
2. **Realization**: design, material, craft requirements high or not; if high craft requirements, a good custom factory generally meets your needs personally.
3. **Consider drawbacks**: cost, eco-friendliness, durability acceptable or not.

No matter which you choose, cabinets consist of design, boards, processing (cutting, drilling, edge banding), accessories (hardware, stone, etc.) and installation.

---

## 2. Design: Plan Storage Needs First

The purpose of design is threefold:
- Size suitable, no wasted space;
- Match living habits, easy to use;
- Look comfortable, match home style.

### Planning you can do yourself
Start from living habits, storage item zoning, embedded appliances, appearance style; find design cases and favorite styles on Xiaohongshu or online, draw a sketch with tools like Kujiale. Plan detailed enough and you may not need a designer.

### What to note when finding a designer
Focus on communication ability and actual cases, ensure they understand your needs, don't just listen to them.

## 3. Boards: Balancing Eco-friendliness and Performance

A board is "substrate + surface"; the substrate is the "filling" inside, the surface is the "skin" outside; performance depends on the material and craft of these two.

### Substrate and surface
- **Substrate**: wood + glue, differing in whether wood is powder, particle, or strip, and the type and amount of glue;
- **Surface**: real wood veneer, paper veneer (melamine paper), plastic laminate, paint, baked paint, wood wax oil. Most common is melamine paper, also called paint-free board.

### How to choose common boards
| Board type | Features | Applicable scenario |
|----------|------|----------|
| Particle board | Wood particles + glue. Average structure, average nail-holding, not moisture-resistant, middle level, cheap | Cabinet body (non-damp places) |
| OSB | Large flakes + MDI glue. Good eco, strong nail-holding, slightly expensive | Wall wood veneer backing |
| MDF | Wood powder (fiber) + glue, easy to carve shapes, but very non-moisture-resistant, much glue, very non-eco | Door panel, back panel |
| Plywood | Wood veneer + glue, good physical performance, moisture-resistant, slightly expensive | Kitchen/bath cabinet body |
| Eco-board | (softwood) wood strips glued into whole board, easy to process, average physical performance | Cabinet body (non-load-bearing places) |
| Solid wood board | Eco and good-looking, expensive, not a whole board but multiple boards + glue, also called finger-joint board | Cabinet door, furniture panel |

### Various terms extended (to avoid being confused by merchants)
"xxx solid wood board", "solid wood xx board" (such as solid wood particle board, plywood solid wood board, eco solid wood board) are meaningless, just for nice sound.
- Solid wood board: finger-joint board and straight-splice board.
- Raw wood board: complete board without splicing (almost impossible to buy on market, ignore).
- Finished board and bare board: finished board has surface applied; bare board has no surface, or randomly applied by some processing factory;
- Reed board: made of reed as main material + glue, popular in recent years, said to be eco, but not yet tested by market.
- Solid wood materials: raw wood and solid wood board.
- Raw wood furniture: no such term now, even if made of raw wood, still called solid wood furniture.
- Solid wood furniture: furniture whose main parts are made of solid wood materials. (small amount of artificial board as auxiliary is still solid wood furniture)
- Pure solid wood furniture: all main and auxiliary materials are solid wood; no such national standard term. National standard only has all-solid-wood furniture and veneered solid wood furniture.
- All-solid-wood furniture: pure solid wood furniture with un-veneered surface.
- Veneered solid wood furniture: pure solid wood furniture with veneered surface.
- Artificial board furniture: made of artificial boards, the first five of the six mentioned above.

### Choose boards by six points
1. **Eco-friendliness**: less glue, more eco. Formaldehyde release national standard: E1 ≤ 0.124 mg/m³, E0 ≤ 0.050 mg/m³, ENF ≤ 0.025 mg/m³;
2. **Stability**: related to substrate structure;
3. **Nail-holding**: depends on wood strength, density, grain;
4. **Moisture resistance**: depends on wood density and surface waterproofing;
5. **Thickness**: usually 18 mm, back panel 9 mm, note unit is mm;
6. **Surface**: surface pattern, grain, surface craft; bare board without surface is called bare board, processing factory applies surface itself; finished board is the one leaving the board factory after surface applied.
7. **Price**: unfolded area or projected area, the latter about four times the former.
8. **Painting**: clear lacquer and mixed paint. The former is transparent paint, the latter is colored paint. To keep wood's original color and grain use clear lacquer, otherwise choose mixed paint.

### Plan selection
- **Not recommended**: all particle board, poor moisture resistance;
- **Recommended**: plywood for kitchen/bath, particle board for others;
- **Also OK**: all cabinet bodies plywood, cabinet doors particle board.

### Surface selection
1. **Melamine surface** (double-surface, melamine board, paint-free board) minimalist, Nordic, log style
   - **Features**: decorative paper impregnated with melamine resin laminated on substrate (e.g. particle board, MDF) surface.
   - **Pros**: wear-resistant, scratch-resistant, high-temperature resistant, chemical corrosion resistant, various colors, economical.
   - **Cons**: door panels usually cannot make complex shapes; poor edge banding craft easily opens and gets damp.
2. **PVC film surface** (blister board, molded board) European, pastoral style
   - **Features**: high-temperature laminated PVC film on MDF or plywood substrate.
   - **Pros**: rich shapes, diverse colors and textures, no edge banding needed.
   - **Cons**: poor high-temperature resistance, not UV-resistant, easily cracks from heat.
3. **Baked paint surface**
   - **Features**: glossy surface formed by multi-layer spray paint and high-temperature baking.
   - **Pros**: high gloss, bright colors, good waterproofing.
   - **Cons**: easily scratched, hard to repair, relatively high cost.
4. **PET surface**: minimalist, Italian style
   - **Features**: a PET film on board surface. Divided into skin-feel PET, high-gloss PET.
   - **Pros**: eco and odorless, rich colors, good touch.
   - **Cons**: not stain-resistant, cannot make complex shapes.
5. **Real wood veneer surface**: new Chinese, Japanese, minimalist
   - **Features**: natural wood veneer on substrate.
   - **Pros**: natural grain, high-end texture, eco.
   - **Cons**: higher cost, needs regular maintenance, sensitive to humidity.

### Brand reference
- **Imported**: Egger, Cleaf, Kronospan;
- **Domestic**: Tutuobao (Rabbit), Qiannianzhou, Moganshan, Lushuihe, Daya, Luhua;
- **Local brands**: look at reputation and history.

## 4. Processing: Good Edge Banding Makes Durable Cabinets

Processing is cutting, drilling, edge banding; edge banding directly affects beauty and eco-friendliness:
- **EVA hot melt**: more glue, easily cracks, obvious glue line;
- **PUR hot melt**: eco, thin glue line, high cost-performance, used more now;
- **Laser edge banding**: best effect but expensive.

Generally obvious glue line means EVA edge banding, but good EVA can also have thin glue line. The most certain proof: EVA edge banding can be torn off in a strip, while PUR cannot.

## 5. Accessories: Hardware and Countertop Must Be Good

### Hardware
Hinges, slides, handles directly affect usage experience; must ask brand and price clearly.
- **Domestic recommended**: DTC, Higo, Topstrong;
- **Imported recommended**: Blum, Hettich, Grass, Salice.

### Countertop
- **Recommended**: only quartz stone recommended, high cost-performance;
- **Optional**: sintered stone, good-looking but expensive, about 2000–3000 yuan/㎡;
- **Not recommended**: stainless steel, for the rich only.

### Sink and basin
- Sink: stainless steel undermount;
- Basin: ceramic integrated, Corian integrated or sintered stone.

## 6. How to Find a Reliable Custom Merchant

1. **Want worry-free**: directly choose Sogal, Oppein these brands;
2. **Want to save money**: the renovation company's channel, control boards and accessories yourself;
3. **Find factory**: search "whole-house customization OEM factory" on map, inspect these four points on site:
   - Is the cutting smooth, no chipping?
   - Is the edge banding smooth, no glue line?
   - Is there a CNC six-side drill?
   - Are eco certificates complete?

### Who is in the industry chain?
- **Board factory**: only produces boards, e.g. Tutuobao, Qiannianzhou;
- **Custom factory**: processes boards into cabinets;
- **Brand**: takes goods from factory, puts own brand, responsible for design, sales, installation;
- **Dealer**: sells brand cabinets, responsible for terminal sales and service.

## 7. How to Deal with Common Add-ons?

Understand the reasonable price of add-ons so you won't be tricked in negotiation:

| Add-on name | Rip-off price | Reasonable price | Online reference | Suggestion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Visible board** | 500 yuan/㎡ | about 1/3 of projected unit price | - | Recommend same material as cabinet door, more coordinated |
| **Milled groove handle-less** | 100–200 yuan/each | 20–50 yuan/each | - | Minimalist style looks good, but bargain |
| **Clothes rod** | 100 yuan/rod | usually free | about 10 yuan/m | Try to get merchant to give, otherwise buy yourself |
| **Imported hinge** | 30–50 yuan/each | about 20 yuan/each | about 20 yuan/each | When upgrading require deducting original hinge cost |
| **Light strip + transformer** | 100–200 yuan/m | 30–50 yuan/m | light strip about 15 yuan/m, transformer about 50 yuan/each | Let factory only groove and install, buy light strip yourself |
| **Drawer** | 200 yuan/each | about 100 yuan/each | slide about 20 yuan/pair | Generally has gift policy, exceed part negotiate at reasonable price |
| **Floor-to-ceiling door** | +200 yuan/㎡ | free within board height ≤ 2400mm | - | Within standard height firmly no extra charge |
| **Straightener** | 150 yuan/rod | about 60 yuan/rod | about 30 yuan/rod | Consider 22mm thick board instead, not necessarily needed |
| **Back panel upgrade** | 200 yuan/㎡ | about 50 yuan/㎡ | - | Generally unnecessary, upgrade takes space |
| **Glass door** | 500–1000 yuan/㎡ | 100–300 yuan/㎡ | about 300 yuan/㎡ | Focus bargaining, negotiate within reasonable range |
| **Top/bottom sealing board** | +300 yuan/㎡ | included in projected area, free | - | Belongs to projected area, should not charge extra |
| **Irregular/extra-deep/extra-high** | double unit price | moderately add by area ratio | - | Confirm price at design stage, avoid passive add-on later |

## 8. Installation and Acceptance

1. Delivered boards placed by zone, not against wall to avoid scratches;
2. Check board brand and color code;
3. Check whether board material matches contract, visible at grooves and holes;
4. Check board thickness, panel and shelf generally 18mm, back panel 9mm;
5. Check edge banding craft qualified, no glue overflow, damage;
6. Check board surface for damage, deformation, warped edge;
7. Check hinges, handles, clothes rods, brand and model match order;
8. Check beauty sealant brand, model match order;
9. Large cartons used to pad split boards, prevent bumps;
10. Excess cartons placed together, sold together at last;
11. Check glass door model, color match order;
12. Check non-standard board size match order, otherwise rework;
13. Tear off labels, check boards for flaws, damage;
14. Check whether waterproof aluminum foil board used under sink;
15. Check cabinet effect matches agreement, e.g. door panel overlap up/down;
16. Check segmented position of upper/lower segments matches order;
17. Check whether left and right cabinet doors are level;
18. Check whether left and right cabinet doors are on one plane;
19. Check edge banding flat, no glue overflow;
20. Check door panel sag, matches order;
21. Check door panel gap even;
22. Check drawer smooth, has damping;
23. Check cabinet door open/close smooth;
24. Check clothes rod installation firm;
29. Check large nail holes all covered with nail hole stickers;
30. Check junction of cabinet body with wall, ceiling straight, even gap;
31. Check junction of cabinet body with wall, ceiling all caulked;
32. Check hinge quantity sufficient;
33. Check special practices match order file;
34. Check balcony cabinet passing AC pipe has holes;
35. Check open grid board junction matches order file;
36. Check suspended cabinet smoke exhaust hole position reserved correctly;

---

Whole-house customization is a system project, from design to installation interlocking. Understand some material and craft knowledge, clarify your needs and budget, to make cabinets that are both good-looking and useful.
