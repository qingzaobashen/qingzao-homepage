# Complete Guide to Selecting Main & Auxiliary Materials in a Full-Package Renovation

## Step 1: Understand the full-package company's playbook first
Before selecting, know three things:
1. **Package materials are standard**: chosen by the company for cost, supply chain, and mass acceptance — meet basic use but not necessarily your wish.
2. **Upgrades are a main profit source**: sales push upgrades. Judge: is this upgrade necessary (waterproofing, wire — core materials) or optional (tile color, wood species)? Like a computer's base config — runs daily software, but big programs need upgrade. Tell "processor-level" core upgrades from "keyboard-film-level" optional ones.
3. **You have the right to know and choose**: the contract must specify every material's brand, series, or model. Vague "first-tier floor" is the root of later disputes.

## Step 2: Bring the list to material selection
Bring this list to the main-material showroom to avoid pitfalls.

### 1. Select materials in this order (photograph brand/model when choosing)
1️⃣ **Auxiliary materials**: pipes, wires, network cable, conduit, putty, latex paint, epoxy grout, wall panels, waterproof coating, cement, gypsum board, ceiling, tile adhesive, wall/ floor primer.
2️⃣ **Main materials**:
🧱 **Floor tiles**: tile/wood floor, bathroom floor & wall tile, bay-window tile/marble, threshold stone, baseboard
🚪 **Doors**: all room doors, kitchen door, bathroom door, shower enclosure
🚾 **Bathroom**: sink, toilet, showerhead, squat toilet, floor drain, faucet
⚒️ **Hardware**: door handles, locks, hinges (flat/ twin/ 3D), angle valves, floor drains, switches, sockets, ceiling panels, lighting (recessed and strip included; spotlights, linear lights self-bought)
🗃️ **Custom**: door fronts, carcass (color, shape, hardware, material), kitchen countertop, sink, faucet;

### 2. Auxiliary materials first — easiest to tamper with
Full-package's easiest corner-cutting is in invisible auxiliary materials — they decide safety and durability, lock them first. Note: all materials need authenticity guarantee and test reports; directly request "show me the actual sample of the material written in the contract."

**Pipes**:
  1. **Wall thickness**: thicker better; cold water ≥3.5mm; hot water ≥4.2mm.
  2. **Texture**: outer wall fine to touch, quality feel.
  3. **Odor**: none; poor ones smell odd.
  4. **Flexibility**: quality pipe bends freely.
  5. **Spec**: PPR, 4.2mm, hot water.
  6. **Brands**: Lianhe (quality), Zhongcai (variety), Rifeng (globally leading), Weixing (large scale).
  7. **Price**: PPR hot water pipe ~13/4m.
  8. **Layers**: single low-end, double mid, triple high-end (some antibacterial).

**Wire**:
  1. **3C certification**: mandatory.
  2. **National standard**: GB/T19666-2005 (flame-retardant sheath).
  3. **Feel**: good wire soft, folds leave no crease.
  4. **Core**: copper, quality core is purplish-red.
  5. **Model**: kitchen/bath 4mm² BV single-strand; living/bedroom 2.5mm² BV — oxidation-resistant, safe.
  6. **Price**: 2.5mm² ~140/roll (100m), 4mm² ~230/roll.

**Network cable**:
  1. Cat5: up to 100M; Cat5e up to 155M.
  2. Cat6: gigabit; Cat6a: 10-gigabit.
  3. Brands: Simon, Schneider, Philips, Legrand, CommScope.

**Conduit**:
  1. **Thickness**: ≥1.2mm, strong enough fingers can't crush.
  2. **Bend test**: insert spring, bend 90°, smooth not flattened/cracked.
  3. **Fire test**: open flame 3–4 times, 20s each, self-extinguish after removal.
  4. Brands: Liansu, Rifeng, Kingbull/Zhongcai, Ginde, Weixing, ERA, Poly.
  5. Price: 3–20/ piece.

**Strong/weak boxes**:
  1. Strong box: count circuits, add spare. Standard bits: 8/12/16/20/24/36; 1P breaker=1 bit, 2P=2 bits, 1P+N RCD usually 2 bits. Small ~12 bits, mid-large ~24.
  2. Weak box: fixed sizes, choose by space.
  3. Brands: YuliKe, Schneider, Delixi, TCL.

**Circuit breakers**:
  1. Models: C10 (lighting), C16 (socket), C25/C32 (AC, water heater), C40 (main).
  2. Usually 1P, 2P; high-power branches (central AC, >6000W) use 2P; others 1P+N.
  3. Brands: ABB, Schneider, Siemens, CHINT, Delixi.

**Cement**:
  1. Use 32.5R, 42.5R.
  2. Check expiry; >3 months unusable, strength drops.
  3. Brands: Conch, South, Huarun, Zhonglian, Jinyu, Zhongcai, Jidong, Shijing.
  4. Price: ~40/bag.

**Waterproof coating**:
  1. Floor: flexible (good elongation, adapts slight structural shift).
  2. Wall: rigid (hard, strong).
  3. Polymer-cement waterproof: GB/T23445-2009.
  4. Membrane under tile adhesive: JC/T2415-2017.
  5. Wall waterproof polymer-cement slurry: JC/T2090-2011.
  6. Two floor coats, one wall coat + a polypropylene fiber layer (scratch-resistant, protects membrane); 48h water-test no leak, then tile.
  7. Brands: Yuhong, Sika, Nippon, DAVCO, 3Trees.
  8. Price: ~150/bucket.

**Epoxy grout**:
  1. Buy from official flagship/authorized stores; verify authenticity and date.
  2. Strong odor = more harmful gas, poor quality; light/no odor = better.
  3. Brands: DAVCO, Pattex, Zhuogao, Sika, Nippon, Zhuanli, Yuhong, Huangshi.
  4. Price: ~80/bottle (400ml).

**Tile adhesive**:
  1. Good water retention, even powder, match grade to tile size.
  2. Brands: DAVCO, Sika, 3Trees, Nippon, Yuhong, Mapei, Meichao.
  3. Price: 35–150/bucket.

**Putty**: reject non-waterproof putty ("putty powder"). Use finished waterproof putty (e.g., Meichao).
  1. **Authenticity**: waterproof; check anti-counterfeit label, scrape coating to call-verify.
  2. **Eco**: GB18582-2020 limits.
  3. **Performance**: JB/T298-2010.
  4. **Waterproof type**: N type; general Y type.
  5. Brands: Meichao, Yuhong, DAVCO, 3Trees, Nippon, Baide, Saint-Gobain, Lions, Bole, Dingguagua.
  6. Price: good waterproof 60–80/bag; ordinary 20/bag.

**Latex paint**: check eco certification, confirm color count (extra charge?).
  1. Buy via proper channels; beware real-bucket fake-paint!
  2. **Label**: domestic has new national standard, import has eco label; more labels = less VOC.
  3. **Contrast ratio**: higher = better coverage.
  4. Brands: Alpina eco good, Flügger eco/quality good, Tikkurila many colors, Dulux & Nippon popular but fakes many.
  5. Price: low 300–500/5L, mid 800–1100/5L, high 1400–2000/5L, art paint 150–400/㎡.

**Gypsum board**:

| Point | Specific standard & check |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Brand & packaging | Known brand (Knauf, Saint-Gobain, Dragon), intact, no damp/damage. |
| Cross-section & pores | Side cut: white dense core, even fine pores; loose/large pores reject. |
| Label & thickness | Verify model, thickness (usual 9.5mm or 12mm). |

Wet areas need water-resistant gypsum board (usually green face paper) with waterproof additive.

**Keel** (the ceiling's load core; strength & rust-proof key):

| Point | Specific standard & check |
|-------|---------------------------|
| Material & galvanizing | Hot-dip galvanized steel strip, even bright zinc, no fall-off/black spots. |
| Thickness | Main keel ≥0.6mm, best 0.8mm; sub & edge ≥0.5mm; measure with caliper. |
| Spec & model | Match design; regular section, no obvious twist. |
| Strength | Light bend: good keel springs back, resists deformation. |

**Silicone**:
  1. Many fakes! especially "Toshiba GE" and "American GE" — verify carefully.
  2. Brands: Wacker, Sika, Pattex, Toshiba EG, Silande, Dow Corning, Baiyun, Anton, DAVCO, Antas, American GE, Changlu.
  3. Price: 30–80/bottle.

### 3. Main materials — in order
Facing pretty showroom main materials, inspect by this order and focus:

#### Tiles
**Spec**:
  1. <100㎡: living floor 800×800mm; kitchen/bath wall 400×800mm, floor 400×400mm.
  2. >120㎡: living 750×1500mm slab better; if budget allows, kitchen/bath wall 600×1200mm.
**Looks**:
  1. Glossy, matte, soft-polished, frosted.
  2. View under natural light; showroom lights too bright.
  3. Coordinate with overall style; ordinary flat — light color, spacious; not dark.
  4. Continuous vein not messy; texture natural/clear; transparent feel; surface flawless, no pinholes/black spots.
  5. No-main-light ceiling → matte/soft, not glossy.
**Packaging**:
  1. Top grade, not qualified.
  2. CCC cert and Class-A radioactivity.
  3. Full-body tile water absorption ≤0.5%, ideally ≤0.1%.
  4. Consistent production date/model, avoid color diff.
  5. Floor tile Appendix G (no fishy smell when mopping); wall tile Appendix H (less hollow/ fall-off).
  6. Real Guangdong tile: address in Guangdong, area code 0757; company/marketing address in Guangdong not necessarily real.
  7. Back logo: "Made in Guangdong, fake pay ten."
**Body & surface**:
  1. Good body: white clean, fine texture, high density, good vitrification, tough. Bad: reddish, short/low-temp fired, poor vitrification. Ultra-white > full-color > cloth > speckle > plain-white > black/red/yellow.
  2. Surface: even glossy glaze, no color diff, no R-angle, stick-print, bubble, white edge.
  3. Feel: smooth not rough, no fingerprint left.
  4. Absorption: drop water on back, watch spread; lower = better.
  5. Flatness: lay several together, smaller seams better.
  6. Hardness: tap center; crisp = high vitrification/density/hardness; dull = inferior.
  7. Wear: scratch surface with hard object; no mark = more wear-resistant. Wear ≥2100 rev, >grade 4.
  8. Stain: oily/marker pen, wipe after minutes; no mark.
  9. Slip: water on surface, feel; static friction >0.6.
  10. Thickness: 800×800 / 600×1200 >9.5mm; 750×1500 >10.0mm.
  11. Weight: 800×800 Guangdong ~46kg/3pcs; non-Guangdong lighter, usually <40kg.

#### Wood flooring
**Solid wood**: one whole wood cross-section, no splice, grain matches. Pros: eco, nice feel, warm/cool, natural grain, insulating. Cons: poor wear, easily damaged/cracked, poor fire resistance, pricey. Price ≥300/㎡. Choose by wood: walnut > teak > oak > ash > cherry > parquet > beech > elm > ash (white) > pine > spruce > rubber > camphor. Thickness ≥18mm.
**Engineered wood (3-ply, new 3-ply, multi-ply)**: multiple layers, top natural wood, core fast-growing/plywood cross-laminated.
- Pure 3-ply: 3 wood layers; top veneer 3–4mm, thicker = pricier. Pros: solid-wood feel, decent cost-performance. Cons: slightly less wear, pricier. Price 210–450/㎡. Veneer ≥1.2mm, >2mm near solid feel.
- New 3-ply: adds diamond wear layer + balance layer. Pros: diamond surface wear/scratch resistant, cost-effective. Cons: diamond surface bubbles if soaked. Price 180–400/㎡. Wear ≥4000 rev.
- Multi-ply: 9–11 wood layers, top like new 3-ply. Pros: wear/scratch, stable, cost-effective. Cons: harder feel, more glue, less eco. Price 150–360/㎡. Eco grade: ENF>E0>E1.
**Laminate**: HDF (wood powder pressed) + decor + wear layer. Pros: wear/scratch, stable, easy care. Cons: hard, poor feel, less eco, poor moisture. Price 80–200/㎡.
**Advice**: pets → ENF diamond-surface engineered (scratch-proof, eco); kids → eco + good feel, high budget solid, cost-performance engineered veneer (pure 3-ply, new 3-ply); rental → laminate; floor heating → feel + eco, pure 3-ply or engineered veneer; lazy → diamond-surface engineered compatible with robot vacuum.

#### Wood doors
Material: painted > film-free; panel: solid multi-ply > engineered; core soundproof: bridge-hole > fiberboard > solid > wood strip > honeycomb; frame: solid > multi-ply > fiberboard.
Thickness: smaller = worse soundproof; choose ≥4.5cm.
Craft: 6-side sealing better moisture-proof; quality door has fine veneer/solid splice, full smooth paint.
Hardware: lock → magnetic silent; hinge → heavy door 3 hinges, flat stronger than twin; seal → brand PU silent strip; door stop → wall stop stronger.
Price & after-sales: clarify quote scope (lock, hinge, stop, install, shipping); width/height limits and overage; install period, payment, warranty.

#### Kitchen sliding door
- Ultra-white glass side is white; ordinary side greenish.
- Easy-clean pretty → wall-track sliding; stable safe → traditional floor-track (plain but practical).
- Frame: ultra-narrow, minimalist premium.
- Floor track: minimalist self-adhesive, clean, robot passes; traditional hard to clean.
- Frame color gray not black (gray easy, black shows dust/fingerprints).
- Aluminum: silicon-magnesium alloy best, durable.
- Wall thickness ≥2.0mm; <1.8mm deforms.
- 3C tempered glass; gray better than white, more premium.
- Kitchen: isolate fume, one layer enough, two excellent.
- Glass ≥8mm, stronger.
- Door & frame same material/color; kids → buffer damper.
- Fiberglass or metal rollers, wear-resistant; avoid plastic.
- Door jamb >16mm, opening 1.5–2m → 3-track, pretty.
- No patterned door (birds/flowers/lines) — cluttered, old.
- Buy branded store with service, not street shop.

#### Bathroom glass door
- Aluminum not wood (wood molds in humidity); aluminum durable.
- Black not white (black versatile, no yellowing; white yellows).
- Single-wrap not double-wrap (single flush with exterior wall, minimalist; double bulky, recessed look, pricier).
- Narrow frame not wide (frame 2–3cm, door ≤2cm; note profile thickness >2.0).
- Oil-sand glass not reeded (oil-sand transmits light not person, matte, wear-resistant, easy; but choose double-sided or see-through when wet); reeded transmits person, poor privacy, hard to clean.
- Single-layer not double (single minimalist, thicker, more styles; double fogs, heavy, fewer profiles).
- Ultra-white not ordinary (low iron, clearer; ordinary greenish).
- 3C tempered not ordinary (tempered safer, honeycomb particles; ordinary sharp shards).
- Whole glass not framed (minimalist); framed lines dated, pricier.
- Magnetic silent lock not ordinary (silent, design, lift to lock, press to open); ordinary noisy, protruding bolt.
- Hidden hinge not ordinary (hidden premium, load-bearing); ordinary exposed.
- Same-color hardware not mixed (frame/handle/hinge unified, black or space-gray; avoid white yellowing; minimalist handle).

#### Shower enclosure
- Not black (looks good but limescale later, hard clean) — chose gun-gray.
- Profile: 304 stainless, moisture/corrosion resistant (ultra-narrow); aluminum rusts in years.
- Glass: ≥8mm explosion-proof tempered (ultra-white; boss said lower burst rate than white), 3C, merchant must apply防爆 film.
- Safety: (1) damping buffer prevents pinch; (2) full frame, safe pretty.
- Rollers: high-speed double-track 8-roller, tight fit, smooth.
- Threshold height: ask installer slightly higher (no splash) but not too high (trip).
- Swing or slide per preference.

#### Sink (hand basin)
- **Ceramic integrated basin** easiest, cost-effective — choose it.
- Custom pretty → slab seamless + ceramic basin.
- Matte premium → artificial stone integrated or slab hot-bent integrated.
- High budget → Corian integrated (only pricey).
- Faucet: pull-out not fixed.
- Mirror cabinet not plain mirror; smart defog.
- Drawer vanity.
- Wall-drain wall-hung not floor-standing (dead corner).
- Paint-free not veneer process.
- Carcass multi-ply not particle board.
- Reserve outlet (charge/hairdryer) and a tissue hole.

#### Toilet
- Siphon not washdown (strong quiet, trap stops odor, saves water).
- 1280°C fired not 800°C (fully vitrified, no crack/leak, long life).
- One-piece not split (no seam, no leak, easy clean).
- Easy-clean glaze not ordinary (fully glazed pipe, no yellow).
- Original quality fittings not cheap (smooth, long life).
- Wider seat not ordinary (even support).
- Pipe >45mm not <45mm (wide, no clog; depth >50mm no splash).
- Soft-close lid not none (slows down, no slam).

#### Squat toilet
- High-temp fired; >1200°C long, high vitrification, dense, heavy.
- Absorption <0.2%, odor/crack proof.
- With trap, anti-odor (and anti-phone, joke).
- Streamlined body, no pooling.
- Anti-slip texture, feel friction.

#### Showerhead
- Body: 59 brass not aluminum (heat/corrosion resistant).
- Color: white or gun-gray (limescale resistant).
- Cartridge: ceramic not wax (heat-resistant, long life, good thermostat).
- Holes: silicone not plastic (easy clean).
- Control: button not rotary (clear, easy).
- Hose: PVC not spiral stainless.
- Head: adjustable not fixed.
- Plus: digital display, spray gun, shelf.

#### Kitchen cabinets
- Countertop quartz not else (unless very high budget for stainless); scratch quartz with metal key → white powder = fake.
- No large grain (messy); pure small grain versatile pretty.
- High-density quartz, oil-proof non-penetrating.
- Thickness 20mm sturdy, chop meat.
- No under-hang (bulky); 45° bevel prettier.
- No right angle (safety); round corner.
- No dark countertop (dark room); light better.
- No backsplash strip (dead corner); counter directly to wall; if strip, rounded.
- Sink height = height/2 +5cm; cooktop = height/2 −5cm.
- Quartz sand >93%.
- Corner staggered not straight-insert.
- Gas stove adds shelf + vents, not none.
- Cabinet backboard moisture-proof, not none; double-sealed 9mm; others 18mm.
- Base cabinet top board for load, not none.
- Cut edges sealed moisture-proof, not none.
- Ordinary double-sided not high-gloss/suede door.
- Carcass multi-ply, door particle or OSB.
- Base no bottom board, embed dishwasher.
- Wrap gas/ drain/ hood pipe, backflow preventer, reserve access.
- Base kickplate aluminum.
- Plastic plug seals holes.
- Under-hang 1cm or hidden slide door.
- Damped hinge.
- Drawers? consider up-fold door.
- Wall cabinet height by hood height.
- Carcass moisture-proof aluminum foil.
- New MS-modified silane sealant: pore-free, non-absorbing, no yellow/mold.

#### Sink (kitchen)
- **Large single bowl** not double (spacious, pots fit; double too small per bowl).
- **304 stainless** (food-grade); 201 rusts, harms health. Real 304 mark is **SUS304**; DUS304/SVS304/USU304 are 201 fakes.
- **Honeycomb water-drop emboss**: wire brush leaves no mark, dirt/wear resistant; mirror sink no protection, scratches/water marks.
- **Sound pad + anti-condensation coat**: bottom coat prevents moisture/mold; pad quiets drain.
- **Drain at side** not center (more under-sink space).
- **Depth >20cm** not <18cm (more, no splash).
- **Panel 4mm** not 1–2mm (sturdy).
- **Rounded inner corners** not right (no dirt hide).
- **Brushed matte**.

#### Faucet
- 304 stainless; copper choose 59A copper <1.9% lead, not zinc alloy.
- Aerator required.
- Finish: 1) brushed anti-scratch dirt; 2) double electroplate hard, acid/alkali resistant, chrome best; 3) electrophoretic gun-gray/white no water stain.
- Pull-out: gravity-ball return, nylon hose quiet wear-resistant.
- Higher, length reaches over drain.
- Handle: light, no resistance = good ceramic cartridge; poor feels resisted.

#### Baseboard
- Prefer surface-mounted, simple practical, minimalist pretty.
- Same color as door frame (clear hierarchy) or floor (extends space, but low ceiling/small caution).
- Same width as door frame, or 4–6cm, harmonious.
- Quality adhesive seal, no yellow/crack.
- Putty to bottom for level, fits seamlessly.
- Same thickness as door frame or 1–2mm less, never protrude.
- Simpler shape prettier, easier clean.
- Bottom caulk, top white ceramic caulk, bottom glass caulk seals gaps.
- 45° miter or corner pieces per need.

#### Hardware
- **Door lock (handle, cylinder, body)**: copper (malleable, many styles); cylinder copper (durable, hard, corrosion-resistant); body magnetic (bolt hidden when open, prevents child injury).
- **Handle**: surface-mounted many options; handle-less: bevel (seal well) or door-bottom extension (cheap); avoid push-open (short life).
- **Hinge (door)**: copper best; 3 pieces steadier; flat strongest; twin cost-effective; hidden (3D) invisible adjustable.
- **Door stop**: wind-proof firm; floor no-drill pretty; wall strong; mind distance to baseboard.
- **Cabinet hinge**: full-overlay for single units; half-overlay shared; inset for embedded; humid (kitchen/bath/balcony) 304 stainless, dry (bedroom/living/study) cold-rolled; rare-open half-damped, frequent quiet full-damped.
- **Slide**: wardrobe side 3-section; heavy load under-mount; kitchen horse-riding. Damped buffer prevents pinch.

#### Switches & sockets
- **Spring**: tin-phosphor bronze > brass-zinc > copper alloy.
- **Panel**: PC > ABS > nylon66.
- **Contact**: silver > copper / iron-plated-silver.
- **Priority**: oblique 5-hole = 7-hole > 5-hole = 4-hole.
- **Power**: high-power 16A 3-hole; others 10A.
- **Per need**: borderless large panel, oblique 5-hole, matte textured, safety shutters; kitchen with switch, bath with splash cover.
- Brands: Bull (quality/price good), Schneider (pretty), Delixi (quality).
- Price: 8–200/ piece by holes/function.

## Step 3: Negotiate and lock
With your list, the strategy differs.
1. **Prioritize upgrading concealed work and high-frequency items**: with limited budget, spend on pipes, wire, waterproofing, hardware hinges, toilet glaze — more worth than tile color.
2. **Use "equivalent swap"**: dislike package tile/floor? don't just hear upgrade price. Ask "can I pick another same-price series?" — often more choice.
3. **Fight "brand upgrade, same price"**: pre-signing is peak leverage. Try "I'm set on you, but hope tile upgrades to XX brand same-price series, or hardware to Blum basic." Sales often applies.
4. **Contract is the only basis**: however pretty the sample, however moving the pitch, only contract text counts. Must write "Tile: XX brand, XX series, model XXX, top grade," "Cabinet carcass: XX brand multi-ply, E0, 18mm."
5. **Photograph as proof**: clear photos of each chosen sample, both sides confirm via WeChat text, as contract attachment.

Selecting full-package main materials is an information war. The more you know, the more cautious they are; the more detailed you ask, the more reliable your future home. With this guide, walk into the showroom calmly — you can absolutely vet for yourself and pick satisfying items within the full-package framework.
