## Hard Renovation Knowledge

### 1. Design and choose the renovation model
1. Bring drawings, budget, and ideas to designers and contractors.
2. Complete design includes: floor plan, construction drawings, renderings; construction drawings cover plan, floor, ceiling, water, electrical, lighting — to guide work.
3. Provide a valid quote based on finalized drawings.
4. When design is finalized, pre-select HVAC, doors/windows, and custom vendors; visit the site together with the designer to confirm HVAC positions and door/window/cabinet sizes.
5. Models: renovation company, design studio, independent foreman, independent designer, self-managed.
6. Compare calmly over time — visit sites, view cases, compare quotes and professionalism.
7. Half-package: foreman + workers do construction; owner controls the whole process and understands content/reason — avoid being fooled; learning renovation is the point.
8. On supervision: most supervisors have limited effect; mastering knowledge and acceptance standards yourself is key.

### 2. HVAC and doors/windows
1. Beyond base construction, HVAC, doors/windows, and custom are pre-decided items usually excluded from company packages.
2. HVAC includes AC, floor heating, water purification; usually one vendor covers all.
3. AC types: split, ducted, central.
4. Floor heating: water or electric; water recommended, electric for occasional use.
5. Fresh-air: ducted for villas, wall-mounted for ordinary homes.
6. Water purification: whole-house filters impurities, softener filters scale, drinking filter kills bacteria; install a backwash pre-filter.
7. Before renovation, usually enclose the balcony or replace old windows.
8. Door/window requirements: insulation, soundproofing, sturdiness (slow heat transfer, good seal, high strength).
9. Window types: sliding (average seal) and casement.
10. Frame material: PVC-steel (good insulation, low strength), aluminum (high strength, poor insulation, rarely used), broken-bridge aluminum (best all-round but pricier).

### 3. Hard renovation steps
1. Basic steps: plumbing/electrical, tiling, carpentry, painting (or plumbing/electrical, carpentry, tiling, painting — "tiling" = "mud").
2. Tiler: backfill, leveling, slope, waterproofing, tile laying.
3. Carpenter: ceilings, door/window finishing, wall wood veneer.
4. Painter: putty, wall paint.
#### 3.1 Start preparation
1. Property filing, renovation deposit, garbage permit.
#### 3.2 Demolition & build
1. Includes demolition, masonry, repair.
2. Protect before demolition with gypsum board/film on areas to keep.
3. If walls aren't waterproof putty, scrape first.
4. Remove insulation wall if poor quality, to save space, or where kitchen/bath need tiles.
5. Window removal timing: after carpentry phase.
6. Masonry material: red brick preferred; if limited, aerated brick + red brick; use rebar planting, toothing, structural columns; old/new wall joined by mortar-tooth technique.
7. After demolition, designer re-measures.
8. Wall/floor primer (yellow wall green floor): locks surface dust, boosts adhesion; apply before work, best after demolition/scraping, before putty/tile; ~100+ yuan/bucket.
#### 3.3 Plumbing/electrical
1. HVAC, doors/windows, custom vendors join the plumbing/electrical handover to set routing and outlet/switch positions.
2. Layout marking: mark all switches/sockets, light lines/troughs, ceiling, curtain box, furniture/appliance positions; demolished/new wall positions, ceiling completion line, door opening line, leveling line, tile line.
3. Water: supply and drain; supply split cold/hot, 25mm PPR pipe, left-hot right-cold; after install, pressure test for no leak.
4. Drain: no clog, no odor; PVC suffices; sizes d110 (sewage), d75 (main drain), d50 (kitchen waste); wall-drain or direct; wall-drain hidden, cleaner; same-floor drainage suggest secondary drain.
5. Electrical: strong and weak current.
6. Weak: network cable Cat6 or above; pre-bury fiber.
7. Strong: BV single-strand hard wire, not BVR multi-strand; sizes 1.5/2.5/4mm²; capacity = size×9 (≤2.5), ×8/7/6 (≥4), e.g., 1.5×9=13.5A, 6×7=42A, ×voltage = max power.
8. Circuit wire sizes: lighting 1.5, ordinary socket 2.5, kitchen/bath 4, ordinary AC 4, central AC 6; ground uniformly 2.5; zero-fire smart switch needs neutral in socket.
9. Conduit: PVC; after completion, test power for continuity.
10. Plumbing/electrical quote: ask for a lump sum by actual design (one price); per-point or per-meter has traps.
11. Routing: run overhead if worried about later repair, underground if confident in quality.
12. Pipes: right-angle elbows; conduits straight; wall wires straight for later locating.
13. Acceptance: photograph plenty after completion for maintenance.
14. Sequence: handover → slotting → drilling → wiring → point install → pre-filter → circuit test → water pressure → embed → floor heating or backfill; ~7 working days.
#### 3.4 Tiling
1. Tiler handles cement work; usually highest cost.
2. Includes walling, pipe wrap, backfill, leveling/slope, waterproofing, tile laying.
3. Bathroom backfill: construction debris, ceramic pellets, foam cement, carbon pellets, or suspended (no fill); foam cement or ceramic pellets recommended.
4. Leveling: no need before tiling (adjust during); after floor heating, have the heating crew level with special concrete.
5. Bathroom sequence: slope first, then waterproof and tile.
6. Waterproof focus: around floor drain; others limited.
7. Waterproof acceptance: fill bathroom with water, check level after 48h.
8. Floor heating floor: engineered wood recommended; laminate varies, solid needs waxing.
9. Tile buying: small-brand high-end products recommended.
10. Tile traits: larger = denser, lower absorption, better, pricier.
11. Wall tile on floor: needs tile adhesive, costlier; ceramic wall tile cheaper but less pretty; merchants suggest floor tile on wall.
12. Tile laying: dry-lay test first, then leveling ruler and levelers for flatness.
13. Wall tile external corner: 45° or bevel (bevel easier to grout).
14. Epoxy grout: recommend twice.
15. Tile quality: watch R-angle (two tiles sink at joint, leaving a stick print after laying).
#### 3.5 Carpentry
1. Main: ceilings and curtain boxes.
2. Material: gypsum and waterproof gypsum board.
3. Keel: kitchen use light steel keel.
4. Schedule: tiling/carpentry ~15 working days.
#### 3.6 Painting
1. Latex paint: big brand, no need for foreign.
2. Process: 2–3 putty coats for leveling, then sand, one primer, two top coats.
3. Crack prevention: at old/new walls and slotted areas, apply mesh before putty.
4. Putty: water-based finished putty recommended.

### 4. Main materials
#### 4.1 Sanitary ware
1. Includes vanity, toilet, showerhead, bathtub.
2. Vanity: choose mirror cabinet for storage.
3. Vanity top: ceramic integrated, Corian integrated, or slab.
4. Toilet: light smart usually enough.
5. Showerhead: white or gun-gray, less scale.
6. Bathtub: Chinese use little, space-hogging, hard to clean — not recommended.
#### 4.2 Cabinets
1. Usually custom, per meter.
2. Countertop: quartz only; not stainless; slab pricey.
#### 4.3 Interior doors
1. Bedroom usually wood door.
2. Wood types: solid and engineered.
3. Engineered structure: solid thin board edging outside, solid wood core inside best.
4. Quality priority: frame core > door panel > frame.
5. Finish rank: painted > lacquered > solid veneer > PVC > melamine paper.
6. Good wood door: frame, panel, frame all solid; best engineered = solid fill + solid veneer.
7. Frame types: single-wrap (one side, other side tiled) and double-wrap (both sides, e.g., bedroom door).
#### 4.4 Baseboard
1. Color: unify with door frame.
2. No-baseboard design: if wanted (costly), discuss with designer early.
3. Material: PVC, bamboo-fiber, metal, tile, solid wood.
   - PVC/bamboo-fiber/metal: clip install.
   - Tile: durable, moisture-proof, less fall-off, but hard to match frame color.
   - Solid: common, invisible-nail install firmer.
#### 4.5 Tiles
1. Spec: <100㎡: living floor 800×800mm, kitchen/bath wall 400×800mm, floor 400×400mm. >120㎡: living 750×1500mm slab better; if budget, kitchen/bath wall 600×1200mm.
2. Color: matte or glossy; view under natural light, showroom too bright.
3. Guangdong tile, full-body water absorption ≤0.5%, dense no seep; identify:
   - Appendix G, no fishy smell when mopping; wall tile Appendix H, less hollow/fall-off.
   - Good body: white clean, fine texture, high density, good vitrification, tough. Bad: reddish, short/low-temp fired, poor vitrification. Ultra-white > full-color > cloth > speckle > plain-white > black/red/yellow.
   - Surface: even glossy glaze, no color diff, no R-angle, stick-print, bubble, white edge.
   - Feel: smooth not rough, no fingerprint.
   - Absorption: drop water on back, watch spread; lower = better.
   - Flatness: lay several, smaller seams better.
   - Hardness: tap center; crisp = high vitrification/density/hardness; dull = inferior.
   - Wear: scratch with hard object; no mark = more wear-resistant. Wear ≥2100 rev, >grade 4.
   - Stain: oily/marker, wipe after minutes.
   - Slip: water on surface, feel; static friction >0.6.
   - Thickness: 800×800 / 600×1200 >9.5mm; 750×1500 >10.0mm.
   - Weight: 800×800 Guangdong ~46kg/3pcs; non-Guangdong lighter, usually <40kg.
#### 4.6 Flooring
1. Laminate: HDF core, melamine decor, alumina wear layer; wear-resistant no-deform; poor moisture, much glue, not for floor heating.
2. Laminate quality: thickness 8–12mm; wear grade national domestic class I (smaller=better) / EU AC3 (larger=better).
3. Engineered wood: multi-ply and 3-ply.
   - Multi-ply: multi-layer core, wood veneer top.
   - 3-ply: top/bottom wood, middle strip.
   - Performance near solid; pros: wood grain, less glue, some moisture resistance; cons: less wear, needs care, pricier.
4. Engineered quality: wood species of core/veneer; veneer thickness (thicker better); wear grade.
5. WPC flooring: wood-plastic, high glue, poor wear, pricey, low recognition.
6. SPC flooring: stone-plastic, tile-like, cost-effective, dense, waterproof, wear-resistant, glue-free, no formaldehyde, fast heat, good for floor heating, thin saves height; cons: corners chip, hard feel, needs self-leveling.
