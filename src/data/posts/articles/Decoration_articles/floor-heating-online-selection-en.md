# Floor Heating Knowledge and Online Screening

## 01 Composition of Floor Heating

### Core Components at a Glance
Think of floor heating as a human body:
* **Boiler (heart)**: Provides the heat source, heats water. **Choose a combi boiler** to meet both heating and domestic hot water needs.
* **Pipes (blood vessels)**: Hot water circulates in them, transferring heat to the floor.
* **Manifold (central nervous system)**: Controls hot water distribution to each room's loop and collects the return water.
* **Temperature control system (reflex nerves)**: Senses room temperature via thermostats, directs the system to run, enabling per-room temperature control.
* **Insulation system (down jacket)**: **The most critical yet most easily overlooked** — it determines whether heat warms your home upward or is wasted downward to the neighbor below. Good insulation can directly reduce later operating costs by over 30%.

### Key Parameters
1. **Boiler**:
   * **Type**: Standard, premix, and condensing boilers. **Condensing boilers** cost a bit more but are more energy-efficient than conventional ones (thermal efficiency > 100%), saving money in the long run. (What is a condensing boiler? It condenses the flue gas after combustion, absorbing the heat in it, while a standard boiler vents it directly.)
   * **Identify authenticity by model number**: A genuine full-premix condensing combi boiler usually has a model number starting with "L", with "Q" as the 5th or 6th character, e.g. LL1GBQ18-B1JG. Prevent being fooled.
   * **Key parameters**:
     * **Power**: Reference formula: **heating area ÷ 10 = minimum required power (KW)**. 18KW: below 90㎡ (heating area); 24KW: 90–150㎡; 28KW: 150–220㎡; 35KW: 220–310㎡.
     * **Minimum heat output**: The smaller the better — low-power heating, the boiler won't start/stop frequently; a good one can reach 3KW.
     * **Domestic hot water volume**: How many liters of water it can raise by 25℃ per minute. The larger the better; **recommend ≥ 12L** to ensure ample and stable shower water.
   * **Brands**: Recommended German Vaillant, Viessmann, Japanese Rinnai, domestic Little Squirrel, Wolf.
2. **Pipes**:
   * **Material**: **PERT pipe** offers high cost-performance for home use; three/five-layer oxygen barrier is best, reducing bacterial growth; main pipes use **PE-X series**; for ultimate, use all PE-X.
   * **Diameter**: Conventionally 16mm or 20mm pipes. For a two-in-one system (floor heating + AC), 20mm pipes are recommended to reduce water flow resistance.
   * **Key requirement**: Must choose **"three/five-layer oxygen barrier"** pipe. It prevents oxygen from penetrating the pipe, breeding bacteria and forming bio-slime, avoiding scaling and clogging of floor heating, affecting heat dissipation.
   * **Brands**: Domestic: Weixing, Poly, Rifeng; joint: Kerno, Waps, Rehau, George.
3. **Insulation material (top priority!)**:
   * **Insulation board**: Recommend **white crystal board (XPS extruded board)**, eco-friendly and odorless, at least 2cm thick, preferably 3cm. Be sure to recognize it; don't let them use ordinary white board. Mushroom board is no good — affects heat dissipation and tends to crack later.
   * **Reflective film**: Must be **pure aluminum mirror reflective film**; its role: balances heat conduction (the thickness of the real aluminum layer determines the effect), the thicker the better, prevents concrete from corroding the insulation board; inferior non-woven reflective film corrodes and fails within months. Where reflective film pieces join, overlap by more than 5 cm and fix with tape.
   * **Boundary insulation strip**: Laid along the wall to prevent heat loss to the wall.
4. **Manifold + temperature control**
   * **Manifold**: Responsible for distributing hot water to each room's loop and collecting return water. Material: prefer integral forged brass, sufficient wall thickness (≥ 4mm), choose high-position or mid-position valve core, less likely to clog. Installation location: prioritize under kitchen sink, under bathroom vanity, etc. near drainage for easy later maintenance.
   * **Temperature control system**: Enables independent per-room temperature control. Install a thermostat panel in each main room, set different temperatures as needed, energy-saving and comfortable.

## Phase 2: Online Research and Screening with One Table

### Step 1: Before Research — Clarify Your Needs

1. **Clarify core needs**:
   * **House situation**: address, building area, **heating area** (deduct areas not paved with pipes such as bathroom, balcony, under floor cabinets).
   * **Functional needs**: heating + domestic hot water combined; need **independent per-room temperature control**.
   * **Hot water needs**: Clarify the number of shower heads and hot water faucets at home (e.g. 2 shower heads + 3 faucets), which relates to boiler hot water capacity selection.
   * **Budget range**: Set a preliminary psychological price range, e.g. 90㎡, around 20k, boiler price accounts for 50–60%.
2. **Brand matrix**:
   * **Wall-mounted boilers**: Vaillant, Viessmann, Bosch, Ariston, Rinnai, Little Squirrel, etc.
   * **Control systems**: Danfoss, Giacomini, Honeywell, Menred, Siemens, etc.
   * **Pipes**: Rehau, Uponor, Georg Fischer, Weixing, Poly, Rifeng, etc.
   * **Insulation auxiliary materials**: Lyu Yu, Dow Chemical, Owens Corning, etc. (**Insulation is key to energy saving, must pay attention**).

### Step 2: Core Tool — "Floor Heating System Configuration Research Table"

Send the table below to interested merchants and ask them to fill it in one by one.

**Floor Heating System Configuration and Construction Detail Research Table**

| Major item | Specific research item | Your questions and reference answers |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Boiler configuration** | **Type and model** | Is it a conventional or **condensing** boiler? Please provide the specific model. (**Tip**: genuine full-premix condensing boiler models usually start with "L", with "Q" as the 5th or 6th character) |
| | **Max/min power** | For 93㎡ heating area, what power is recommended? What is the **minimum output power**? (The smaller, the more stable hot water, the more energy-saving heating) |
| | **Brand** | Which specific brand and series? |
| | **Hot water per minute** | At 25℃ rise, how many liters of hot water per minute? **Recommend ≥ 12L** to ensure comfort with multiple water points. |
| | **Price** | What is the unit price of the boiler equipment part? |
| **2. Manifold** | **Material and craft** | Is it **integral forged brass**? Is wall thickness ≥ 4mm? |
| | **Brand** | What brand? (e.g. Danfoss, Giacomini, Menred) |
| | **Valve core** | Is the valve core high, mid, or low position? (High position less likely to clog) |
| | **Flow meter** | Does it have a visual flow meter? (for balance adjustment and diagnosis) |
| **3. Temperature control** | **Thermostat/actuator brand** | What brand for per-room control? Does it support phone APP control? |
| **4. Pipe system** | **Main/loop pipe material** | Is the main pipe PEX? Is the loop pipe **PERT five/three-layer oxygen barrier**? (oxygen barrier prevents scaling) |
| | **Brand and spec** | What brands respectively? What diameter? (e.g. 20mm or 16mm) |
| **5. Insulation structure** | **Insulation board** | What brand and material of insulation board? (**Must be white crystal board/XPS extruded board**, thickness ≥ 2cm) |
| | **Reflective film** | Is it **pure aluminum mirror reflective film**? |
| | **Boundary strip etc.** | Does it include **boundary insulation strip**, **silicon crystal mesh**, **aluminum foil tape**? |
| **6. Construction craft** | **Loop laying** | What is the loop spacing? (standard 15–20cm) Is the floor part **a whole pipe with no joints**? |
| | **Expansion joint** | When area > 30㎡ or length > 6m, **is an expansion joint set**? |
| | **Backfill material** | What material for backfill? **Must be pea-gravel concrete** (cement:sand:pea gravel ≈ 1:2:3). |
| | **Pressure test** | **Is a pressure test done**? What pressure? How long held? (standard: 0.6–0.8 MPa, held until end of renovation) |
| | **Construction standard** | What specification is construction based on? (e.g. JGJ142-2012) |
| **7. Price and after-sales** | **Material + labor unit price** | Except boiler, **what is the comprehensive unit price per ㎡ of heating area**? (includes all pipes, auxiliaries, labor) |
| | **Total price composition** | Please list: boiler price + heating area unit price × area = final total. |
| | **Warranty policy** | Boiler, pipes, manifold, thermostat, installation — **how long is each warranted**? |

### Step 3: Analyze Replies and Compare 3 Merchants

After receiving several replies, compare and analyze horizontally, focusing on differences:

1. **Prioritize eliminating the unprofessional**:
   * Those who avoid the table content and only push you to talk offline.
   * Those who describe **insulation materials** (white crystal board, pure aluminum film) and **construction details** (expansion joints, pea-gravel backfill) vaguely.
   * Those with **abnormally low quotes**. Low price must be compensated from products (e.g. non-oxygen-barrier pipes, inferior insulation board) or craft (fewer construction steps).
2. **Key item comparison**:
   * **Boiler**: Compare **minimum output power** and domestic hot water output rate, which directly affect experience.
   * **Insulation**: This is the core of later energy saving. Compare insulation board brand, thickness, whether reflective film is pure aluminum.
   * **Construction**: Compare backfill material ratio, pressure test commitment, etc.
   * **Price**: Break the total into "boiler equipment fee" and "per-㎡ laying fee" for clearer comparison.
3. **Online initial communication feel**:
   * Is the customer service or sales patient and professional in answering your questions?
   * Do they proactively invite you to see the site or company qualifications?
   * How smooth is communication? This affects later cooperation.

After completing the online table research and initial communication, our goal is not to place an order online, but to **screen out 2–3 most reliable merchants and enter the offline inspection phase**.
