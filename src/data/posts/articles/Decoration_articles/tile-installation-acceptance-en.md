# Tile Installation and Acceptance

## 1. Preparation Before Construction: Confirm the Layout Drawing

1. Confirm the tile layout drawing:
   - This is a key basis for construction. Based on the drawing, confirm the starting point, joint width, and correspondence with door openings and cabinets, and optimize the hiding spots for small and leftover tiles (e.g. under furniture, in inner corners).

---

## 2. Supervision Points During Construction

1. Clean the tile release agent: Before laying, check the back of the tiles. If there is a white powdery release agent, clean it thoroughly with a wire brush to ensure a firm bond.
2. Use professional tools for openings: All openings for water pipes and sockets should use a dedicated hole saw to ensure smooth and precise holes, avoiding tile cracking from violent knocking.
3. Clean and groove promptly: Require the worker to wipe off cement residue on the tile surface at any time during laying, and groove the joints before the grout dries, laying a good foundation for later beauty-sealing (epoxy grout).
4. Full-house continuous laying, use threshold stones with caution:
   - Full-house continuous tile laying makes the space look larger and more coherent. Except for wet zones, try to eliminate threshold stones. At doorways, use whole tiles or large tiles cut after calculation, avoiding narrow "small strip tiles."
5. Bevel (hai tang) corners on wall tile external corners:
   - All wall tile corners (external corners) are recommended to be made into "hai tang corners" (leave a 2mm chamfer), not the fragile 45° sharp corners. The gap left by the hai tang corner, after beauty-sealing, looks good and is not prone to chipping.
6. Bathroom construction points:
   - Slope: Use a laser level to assist construction, ensure the floor drain is the lowest point of the whole area, and a water-pour test shows rapid drainage with no pooling.
   - Wall-floor alignment: The joints of wall tiles and floor tiles should be aligned as much as possible to enhance visual integrity.
   - "回"-shaped floor drain: Cut the tile at the floor drain into a "回"-shaped (square ring) pattern to accelerate drainage.
   - Shower area sunken: The shower area floor should be 2–3 cm lower than the dry area to achieve natural water blocking.
   - Single-wrap door opening: Tile the inner side of the door opening, only do the outer door casing, cleaner and more beautiful.
7. Kitchen construction points:
   - Wall squaring and leveling: This is the premise of installing a countertop without a backsplash strip; use a straightedge to carefully check wall flatness and verticality.
   - Backdraft damper on whole tile: The range hood backdraft damper should be installed by opening a hole in one whole tile to ensure a tight seal.
   - Use leftover tiles cleverly: Areas completely blocked by cabinets can use leftover tiles to save cost.
8. Window opening pre-treatment:
   - Before tiling, use an electric hammer to chisel out depth and slope on the inner side of the window opening, so that the later window casing installation fits better.

## 3. Post-completion Acceptance Standards

Golden timing for acceptance: before the tiler completes floor protection!

| Acceptance item | Inspection tool and method | Pass standard and problem handling |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1. Hollow rate check | Tap the four corners and center of the tile with a hollow-spot hammer (or metal spoon). | Wall tiles: single tile hollow rate ≤ 5%, overall ≤ 5% is pass.<br>Floor tiles: no hollow allowed, especially at edges and corners.<br>Handling: edge/corner hollow can be injected with grout; center or large-area hollow needs replacement. |
| 2. Surface flatness | 2-meter straightedge + feeler gauge. Place the straightedge against wall/floor, measure gap with feeler. | Gap ≤ 2 mm is pass (national standard). Focus on checking whether tile-to-tile joints are flat. |
| 3. Verticality and corners | Infrared level, right-angle ruler. | Wall vertical deviation ≤ 3 mm. Internal/external corners should be 90°, deviation ≤ 3 mm. |
| 4. Joint height difference | Finger touch, slide coin edge across joint. | Tile joints should be flush, no obvious scratchy feel, coin slides smoothly. |
| 5. Slope and drainage | Ping-pong ball, water-pour test. | Place ping-pong ball on bathroom floor, it should roll naturally to floor drain. No pooling after pouring water. |
| 6. Appearance and damage | Visual inspection, strong flashlight side light. | No chipping, cracking, obvious color difference, scratches. Joints uniform, no cement residue. |
| 7. Functional holes | Visual. | Floor drain, water pipe interfaces have smooth round holes, proper size, no large gaps after installation. |

> Special tip: Bring small tools like a coin and ping-pong ball for quick tests during acceptance.

---

## 4. Work After Acceptance

1. Finished product protection: After passing acceptance, urge the contractor to cover the floor with professional thickened protective film and seal the edges with tape to prevent scratches and contamination from later construction.
2. Photo documentation: Photograph the whole-house tile laying effect, especially the tile areas with water/electric line routing, for archive, to facilitate avoiding pipes when installing cabinets and sanitary ware later.
