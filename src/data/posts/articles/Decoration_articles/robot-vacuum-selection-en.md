# Robot Vacuum Selection Guide

### Part 1: Cleaning Ability

Cleaning ability is the foundation of a robot vacuum.

| Selection dimension | Want √ | Don't × | Key analysis & suggestion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Anti-tangle ability** | **Main brush has anti-tangle design** (e.g. rubber-bristle combo roller, pure rubber brush or special anti-tangle structure). | **Traditional brush without anti-tangle design**. | **This is the primary pain point!** For long-hair, pet households, brush tangling is the biggest trouble. Prefer rollers with active cutting or special structural design. |
| **2. Suction power** | **Suction ≥ 9000Pa**, **current mainstream mostly above 10000Pa**. | **Suction < 9000Pa** outdated or low-end models. | Suction determines recovery efficiency of dust, particles. Larger suction, stronger cleaning, especially carpet. |
| **3. Corner cleaning** | **With "mechanical arm" or "dynamic side brush"** extension cleaning tech. | Only basic wiggle side brush. | Traditional round body cannot fully hug wall, forming dead corners. **Mechanical arm tech** lets side brush or mop extend for true edge cleaning, a game-changing feature. |
| **4. Dust collection method** | **Dual side brushes**. | **Single side brush**. | Dual side brushes gather garbage to main suction more efficiently, fuller coverage, higher efficiency. |

### Part 2: Smart Navigation and Obstacle Avoidance

No matter how strong the suction, if it often "acts silly", gets stuck or misses spots, it's meaningless.

| Selection dimension | Want √ | Don't × | Key analysis & suggestion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **5. Obstacle avoidance tech** | **Binocular vision avoidance** or **structured light + AI vision fusion avoidance**. | **Mechanical collision avoidance** (basically eliminated) or **infrared avoidance** (low precision). | **Avoidance ability sets the experience floor**. Excellent avoidance precisely identifies slippers, socks, wires, pet feces, avoiding pushing, tangling or "spreading feces" tragedy. **Binocular vision is current high-end mainstream**. |
| **6. Navigation and mapping** | **LDS laser radar navigation** (mainstream mature) or **3D TOF radar navigation** (higher precision). | **Visual navigation** (strong light dependence, poor stability) or irregular random collision. | Laser navigation maps fast, high precision. High-end TOF performs better in dark. Accurate map and path planning is the basis of efficient whole-house coverage. |
| **7. Obstacle crossing** | **Crossing height ≥ 20mm**. | Weak crossing (< 15mm). | Ensure robot easily crosses sliding door tracks, bathroom thresholds and other common low obstacles. |

---

### Part 3: Base Station Functions

Base station functions determine how often you need to "serve" it, the core of high automation.

| Selection dimension | Want √ | Don't × | Key analysis & suggestion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **8. Auto dust collection** | **Base with auto dust collection**, large dust bag (recommend > 2L). | **No auto dust collection**. | **Core hands-free function**. After each cleaning, robot auto pumps dust box garbage to base's large dust bag; you only **replace dust bag monthly or even every few months**, completely saying goodbye to frequent dirty dust box cleaning. |
| **9. Auto mop washing** | **Base with hot water wash, scrub, dry mop function**. | Manual mop washing or only simple wetting. | Auto wash and dry mop, avoid odor and bacteria. **Hot water wash and scrub** far superior to cold water rinse. |
| **10. Auto water in/out** | **Base supports auto water in/out module** (needs reserved space). | Only supports manual tank water change. | **Ultimate hands-free function**. Like washing machine, auto adds clean water, drains wastewater, achieving months of no manual intervention, especially for large flats. |
| **11. Battery capacity** | **≥ 5200mAh** (for 60㎡+ units). | Too small, needs mid-recharge. | Large battery ensures one-time whole-house cleaning, improves efficiency. |

### Part 4: Brand Choice

Different brands differ significantly in tech route and product features.

| Brand | Pros √ | Cons × | Suggestion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Roborock** | **Outstanding anti-tangle tech**, stable algorithm, excellent navigation planning. | Some models had "**easy water intake, floor scratching, loud dust collection, frequent fan anomalies**" (watch if new models improved). | **Recommended**: first choice for those valuing **hair anti-tangle**, algorithm stability. Focus on latest flagship or main models. |
| **Ecovacs** | High market share, rich product line, some models lead in AI avoidance. | Some models have "**bad mapping, dumb, can't return to base, water marks, high repair cost**" negative feedback, quality control and algorithm stability reputation divided. | **Suggestion**: watch high-end flagship (e.g. X2 series) but carefully check real users' long-term reviews; mid-low models cautious. |
| **Dreame** | Parameter "roll king", **complete feature config** (suction, hot water wash, mechanical arm), high cost-performance. | May have "**complete features but none refined**", overall tuning, algorithm details and long-term stability need further market test. | **Recommended**: for "tech early adopters" pursuing **high parameters, new features** with limited budget. |
| **Narwal** | Early pioneered "auto mop wash", mopping experience once praised. | **"Weak suction, frequent dust box cleaning"**, and "**mall counters, poor quality control**" evaluations. | **Cautious**: today with competitors fully developed, its core advantage no longer obvious, and many shortcomings exposed, recommend prioritizing other brands. |

### Selection Checklist

**Step 1: Clarify needs**
* Many hairs at home? Prefer Roborock.
* Hate emptying? Choose base with auto dust collection.
* Want total hands-off? Auto water in/out is the ultimate choice.
* Unit size determines battery and endurance needs.

**Step 2: Check key parameters**
1. **Basic config**: suction above 10000Pa, laser/TOF navigation, binocular or AI vision avoidance.
2. **Solve pain points**: anti-tangle main brush, mechanical arm edge cleaning.
3. **Hands-free**: auto dust collection, hot water wash and dry mop.

**Step 3: Choose brand**
* **Stable, good hair cleaning**: Roborock flagship or sub-flagship.
* **Good avoidance, want to try**: watch Ecovacs latest flagship user feedback.
* **High cost-performance, new features**: Dreame top config model.

**Final advice**: Good parameters don't necessarily mean good use. After selecting 1–2 online, must check real users' 3+ month reviews, focusing on:
* Is avoidance still accurate after long use?
* Do roller and side brushes easily tangle hair?
* Does base easily smell, easy to clean?
* Is the APP smooth, stable?
