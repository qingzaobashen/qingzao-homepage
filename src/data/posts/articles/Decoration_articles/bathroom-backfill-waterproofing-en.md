# Bathroom Waterproofing and Backfill Process Guide

The bathroom is the place with the highest water hazard risk and the largest rework cost in the home, especially the sunken bathroom. Poor waterproofing and backfill affects downstairs neighbors, damages your own walls, and affects future use experience. Standard construction process is the key to eliminating leakage and settlement.

## 1. Backfill Methods and Waterproof Materials

### 1.1 Backfill Materials and Pros/Cons
![Backfill materials](/images/articles/bathroom-backfill-waterproofing/1-回填材料.jpg)
The sunken bathroom (sump) has pipes under the floor slab, usually needs backfill to level the ground and protect pipes.

**1. Construction Waste Backfill**
- Practice: directly use renovation debris such as bricks, concrete fragments for filling.
- Pros: extremely low cost, easy to source.
- Cons: material heavy, increases floor load, easily causes settlement; sharp fragments easily damage waterproof layer and pipes, causing leakage; large gaps easily accumulate sewage, breed bacteria and odor.
Currently widely regarded as a backward and not recommended practice.

**2. Charcoal Slag Backfill**
- Practice: use charcoal slag for filling; charcoal slag is the product of incomplete wood combustion, with dense structure.
- Pros: cheap, light, no excessive burden on floor slab, small particles, won't hurt waterproof layer and pipes, good water absorption, uniform density.
- Cons: soft material, must fill and press, still has settlement risk.

**3. Ceramsite Backfill**
- Practice: use light, porous round ceramsite for filling, and lay steel mesh on surface for reinforcement.
- Pros: light material, no excessive burden on floor slab; good water absorption, helps keep dry, small and round particles, won't hurt waterproof layer and pipes.
- Cons: higher material and construction cost; high craft requirement, if no steel mesh reinforcement, ceramsite easily shifts and sinks, causing ground cracking.

**4. Elevated Backfill (Precast Panel Elevated)**
- Practice: use bricks to build support piers, then cover with precast cement board, forming an elevated layer, then do waterproofing and tiling on the cement board.
- Pros: lightest overall weight; hollow inside, convenient for later pipe repair; good drainage.
- Cons: relatively complex construction, high cost; poor sound insulation; if waterproofing fails inside elevated layer, easily accumulates water vapor, may breed insects and ants.

**5. Foamed Cement (Backfill Treasure) Backfill**
- Practice: use special foamed cement material poured on site, its fluidity automatically fills all gaps.
- Pros: light texture, good fluidity, can wrap pipes into a whole, seamless; has certain waterproof and sound insulation performance.
- Cons: highest material and labor cost; once cured, extremely difficult to repair, need whole chiseling.

### 1.2 Waterproof Materials and Pros/Cons
![Waterproof materials](/images/articles/bathroom-backfill-waterproofing/2-防水材料.jpg)
Waterproofing usually needs two layers: once at the bottom of the sump before backfill, once on the surface after backfill leveling.
1. Flexible waterproof coating (common)
- Practice: brush polyurethane, acrylic or polymer cement (K11) and other flexible waterproof materials on the leveled base, generally brush 2–3 times, focus on corners, pipe roots and other joints.
- Pros: seamless connection, adapts to slight cracking and deformation of base; convenient construction.
- Cons: high requirement for base flatness and cleanliness; brush thickness not easy to uniform, if too thin easily fails.

2. Membrane waterproofing
- Practice: lay waterproof membrane (e.g. SBS, polypropylene fabric membrane), bonded to base by hot melt or cold adhesive.
- Pros: uniform thickness, stable waterproof performance; fast construction.
- Cons: at complex parts such as pipes, corners need cutting and splicing, improper handling easily becomes leakage points; high requirement for construction tech.

## 2. Standard Construction Steps
![Waterproof construction](/images/articles/bathroom-backfill-waterproofing/3-防水施工01.jpg)
![Waterproof construction](/images/articles/bathroom-backfill-waterproofing/4-防水施工02.jpg)

**Step 1: Base Treatment**
Base inspection and repair: after plumbing/electrical renovation, clean sump to solid base. Check wall-floor junction, around pipe roots for voids, cracks, carefully brush all internal corners with leak-sealing king.

**Step 2: First Waterproof Coating**
First waterproof coating: do first waterproof on whole sump bottom ground, wall return height 30–40 cm. This layer prevents structural leakage and masonry wall root water absorption.

**Step 3: First Water Retention Test**
After waterproof layer dries, store water in sump, depth not less than 3 cm, continue 24 hours. Must go to downstairs neighbor's home to see corresponding ceiling, confirm no leakage, water stain, wet mark. Only after passing can proceed to next step.

**Step 4: Red Brick Grid**
Build "well"-shaped grids with red bricks in the sump. Divide backfill area into several independent small units, disperse ground load, avoid overall settlement.

**Step 5: Fill Ceramsite**
Fill ceramsite in red brick grids. Ceramsite porous, light, low water absorption, reduces floor burden, can also store and drain water, ideal backfill material. Don't use construction waste backfill.

**Step 6: Lay Steel Mesh (Enhance Integrity)**
After ceramsite layer leveled, lay steel mesh about 6mm diameter, grid spacing 15–20 cm. Steel layer pulls the upper cement mortar leveling layer into a whole, prevent cracking, sinking.

**Step 7: Cement Mortar Leveling**
Pour cement mortar leveling on steel mesh, make solid base. Mortar grade appropriate, too high easily cracks, too low insufficient strength, sandy.

**Step 8: Second Surface Waterproofing (Core Waterproof Layer)**
After leveling layer dries do second waterproofing, this is the key step.
- Wall: shower area waterproof height not less than 1.8 m, other areas not less than 30 cm, brush twice.
- Ground: also brush twice, form a whole with wall waterproof.
- Details: floor drain, pipe root, internal/external corners and other weak places focus brush one more time.

**Step 9: Second Water Retention Test (Final Acceptance)**
This is the "big exam" of waterproofing. Store water again 24–48 hours, recheck downstairs. Only after passing is waterproofing truly qualified.

**Step 10: Make Waterproof Protective Layer**
After waterproof layer acceptance qualified, immediately use thin cement mortar as protective layer. Prevent subsequent tiling, sharp tools or sand from scratching waterproof layer.

**Step 11: Wall Scratch Treatment (Ensure Firm Tiling)**
Waterproof layer surface too smooth, direct tiling easily hollow and fall off. Must do scratch treatment (use cement mortar for rough surface), increase wall and tile adhesive grip.

## 3. Key Supervision Points and Common Traps

| Key link | Must supervise and check | Common trap and consequence |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Material arrival** | Check waterproof coating brand, model, shelf life; check ceramsite clean no debris. | Use inferior waterproof coating or non-breathable rigid waterproof; use construction waste instead of ceramsite. |
| **Water retention test** | Yourself or property go downstairs to check, mark water level line. | Contractor checks itself and lies about result, leaving leakage hidden danger. |
| **Backfill craft** | Confirm red brick grid, steel mesh laid. | Directly pour ceramsite, no grid no steel, high settlement risk. |
| **Detail treatment** | Check pipe root, wall corner with leak-sealing king reinforcement, waterproof brushed evenly? | Sloppy detail treatment, becomes future leakage "breakthrough". |
| **Wall scratch** | After completion touch wall, must have obvious rough feel. | Skips this step for convenience, later large-area tile hollow and fall off. |

## 4. Notes

1. Keep evidence in process: ask contractor to photo or video key processes (especially water retention test), send to you for archive, as basis for supervision and rights protection.
2. Don't save time and material: bathroom waterproofing backfill is "hidden project within hidden project", being cheap or rushing to simplify steps, future repair cost may be tens of times the saved, and affects neighbor relations.
3. Choose professional team: propose these craft standards to contractor, write into contract attachment. Professional team will proactively comply with these specs.
