# Ducted Air Conditioner Selection and Installation Guide

## Selection Phase: Clarify Your Needs

![Ducted AC selection](/images/articles/central-ac-selection-install/3-风管机挑选.jpg)
### 1. Determine Core Parameters: Capacity and Efficiency
* **Cooling capacity (tonnage)**: Choose based on room area, lean larger rather than smaller. Refer to the table below; for west-facing, large glass windows, or high ceilings, choose slightly larger.
* **Energy efficiency grade**: Prefer the new APF Grade 1 (top tier). A bit more expensive, but more power-saving in the long run.

| Tonnage (P) | Reference cooling capacity (W) | Applicable area (㎡) | Applicable scenario |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1.5P | ~3500W | 10–18 | Small living room, large bedroom |
| 2P | ~5000W | 18–28 | Regular living room |
| 3P | ~7200W | 28–35 | Large living room, open layout |
| 4P/Large 3P | 7500W+ | 35–50+ | Extra-large combined living-dining |

### 2. Functional Needs and Brands
* **Control method**: Ask clearly whether the standard is a basic button wired controller or a smart wired controller (usually extra cost). Smart ones allow phone remote control, much more convenient.
* **Noise level**: If sensitive to sound, ask how many decibels the indoor unit runs at.
* **Condensate lift pump**: If the installation location is far from the drain outlet, or the ceiling is thin and cannot make the drainage slope (needs 1%), you must install a lift pump, otherwise it will leak and soak the ceiling. Ask if there is a float switch (anti-leak protection).
* **Brand choice**: Based on budget and needs:
  * High-end: Daikin, Mitsubishi Electric — stable technology, high price.
  * Mid-high/domestic: Gree, Midea, Haier, Hitachi — high cost-performance, large market share.
  * High cost-performance: Hisense, Aux, Hua Ling, etc.

### 3. Fee Negotiation: Ask About Hidden Items
When talking to the merchant, write these costs into the quote to avoid later additions:
1. **Copper pipe cost**: How many meters does the standard installation package include? How much per extra meter?
2. **Air outlet cost**: How long is the standard outlet? How much per meter for an extended linear outlet?
3. **Hole drilling and groove cutting fee**: Is cutting wall holes and grooving for the wired controller extra?
4. **High-altitude work fee**: Is there this fee for installing the outdoor unit on high floors?
5. **Power and wiring**: Does it include the signal wire from controller to indoor unit? The AC power line (recommend a dedicated 4 sq mm line) is usually reserved by the electrician — coordinate in advance.

## Installation Preparation: Material Acceptance and Positioning

### 4. Delivery and Material Acceptance
* **Verify model**: Check that the model on the outer packaging and the nameplates of indoor/outdoor units matches the contract.
* **Check appearance**: See if the machine has bumps or deformation.
* **Inspect auxiliary materials**: This is very important! Require brand-specific auxiliary material kits, or confirm these materials:
  * **Refrigerant copper pipe**: Use dephosphorized seamless copper pipe, wall thickness at least 0.8mm, bright color.
  * **Insulation foam**: Thickness at least 15mm, fast rebound when pressed, flame retardant grade B1 or above.
  * **Condensate pipe**: Use PVC-U water supply pipe, high hardness, no deformation.
  * **Hangers, pipe clamps**: Use all-metal (e.g. galvanized), sturdy and rust-proof.

### 5. Indoor Unit Positioning and Protection
* **Installation position**: The indoor unit must not be tight against the ceiling; leave return-air and maintenance space, at least 2–3 cm from the top. Fix with double nuts + damping pads to reduce noise.
* **Dust protection**: After hoisting, wrap the indoor unit's air outlet and return inlet with the dust cover provided, seal tightly with tape, so renovation dust does not get in.

## Installation Process: Core Craft Supervision

![Ducted AC installation](/images/articles/central-ac-selection-install/4-风管机安装.jpg)
### 6. Pipe Construction
* **Neat and straight**: Refrigerant pipes and condensate pipes should be laid neat and straight. Use a dedicated bender for copper pipe bends, not by hand, which easily crushes them.
* **Nitrogen brazing**: When brazing copper pipes, purge with nitrogen to prevent internal oxidation, otherwise welding slag will damage the compressor.
* **Drainage slope and vent**: The condensate pipe needs a downward slope greater than 1%, with a vent at the highest point, ensuring smooth drainage without noise. Do a water-fill test after installation.
* **Tight insulation**: All copper pipe, condensate pipe joints, and valves must be tightly wrapped with insulation foam and glued with special tape to prevent condensate.

### 7. Electrical and System Commissioning
* **Vacuum pumping**: After connecting the system, use a vacuum pump to evacuate for at least 20 minutes (depending on pipe length), to remove air and moisture. This step is important.
* **Power-on test**: Test cooling/heating on site, check if the wired controller functions well, listen for any strange noise from the indoor unit.

## Final Acceptance and Follow-up Coordination

### 8. Installation Finish
* **Hole sealing**: Fill all wall-penetrating holes with foam, especially exterior walls, to prevent rain and insects from entering.
* **Mark dimensions**: After installation, ask the worker to mark the size and position of the air outlet, return inlet, and access panel on the dust cover, for the carpenter to cut the ceiling opening.
* **Part custody**: After the test passes, remove the wired controller and keep it yourself; install it after the wall decoration is done, so it doesn't get dirty or damaged.

### 9. Coordination with Renovation Steps
1. **Plumbing/electrical phase**: Ask the electrician to reserve a dedicated AC power line (4 sq mm). Based on the merchant's given depth, groove and embed the junction box for the wired controller.
2. **Ceiling phase**: Tell the carpenter the outlet size to ensure accurate opening. After the ceiling is done, ask the merchant to measure and customize the outlet panel.
3. **Final installation**: After wall painting and cleaning, notify the worker to install the outlet panel and wired controller, and do the final commissioning.
