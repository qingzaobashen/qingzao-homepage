# Painter Acceptance Guide

## 1. Core Principle: Phased Acceptance

Painter acceptance cannot only be checked after the final latex paint is brushed. It should be accepted at two important nodes:
1. Phase 1: After putty sanding ends, before latex paint — this is the best time to check wall flatness.
2. Phase 2: After latex paint fully dries, before furniture enters — this is the time to check final finish effect and function.

---

## 2. Phase 1 Acceptance: After Putty Sanding (Check Flatness)

At this time the wall is pure white putty layer, any unevenness is fully exposed. Once latex paint is brushed, especially on colored walls, uneven problems are covered, extremely hard to find.

### Acceptance Time and Environment:
- **Time**: at night.
- **Environment**: turn off indoor main light, create dark environment.

### Core Acceptance Method: Side Light Illumination
1. **Prepare tool**: high-brightness flashlight or iodine-tungsten lamp.
2. **Operation**: place light source close to wall, illuminate parallel along wall.
3. **Pass standard**: under side strong light, wall should show even light-shadow transition, no obvious waves, bumps, sandpaper marks.
4. **Problem sign**: if water-ripple-like light-dark stripes appear, it's "wavy wall", meaning sanding unqualified.

### Check Other Items Synchronously:
- **Internal/external corners**: use right-angle ruler to check 90 degrees, straight.
- **Flatness**: use 2-meter straightedge against wall, feeler to check gap. National standard requires smooth-flat error ≤ 4mm, but for better effect (especially where custom cabinets installed), strive ≤ 3mm.
- **Details**: check around switches/sockets, baseboard position, door/window frame edges, whether putty finished clean, flat.

If "wavy wall" or serious unevenness found at this phase, must require worker to re-sand until qualified, otherwise cannot enter painting!

---

## 3. Phase 2 Acceptance: After Latex Paint Done (Final Effect Check)

After latex paint fully dries (usually one week after brushing), do final acceptance.

### 1. Check Flatness and Smoothness (Re-check)
- **Side light re-check**: even if putty phase passed, painting may produce sags, brush marks. Again observe wall under daytime natural side light.
- **Touch check**: back of hand lightly stroke wall, feel smooth, no particles, burrs.

### 2. Check Wall Strength (Prevent Powdering)
- **Friction test**: use clean dark cloth or sock, rub wall back and forth a few times with some force.
- **Pass standard**: almost no or only very slight white dust on cloth. If serious powdering, means putty strength insufficient or latex paint adhesion poor, future easily dirty and unwashable.

### 3. Check Appearance and Craft Details
- **Color difference**: stand in room center, observe whether wall color uniform, no show-through, missed brush.
- **Sags and lumps**: check wall especially internal corners, whether paint "tear"-like protrusions dripped down, or un-dissolved paint lumps.
- **Cracking**: carefully check gypsum board joints, old/new wall junctions, wire-groove backfill, whether tiny cracks.
- **Edge finishing**: check junction with ceiling, baseboard, door/window frames, cabinets, whether paint line straight, clear, no pollution.

### 4. Check Functional Areas
- **Baseboard position**: confirm lowest latex paint height on wall, whether below planned baseboard height, avoid exposing unpainted wall after installation.
- **Around switches/sockets**: clean no pollution, neat edge cutting.

## 4. Common Problems, Causes and Handling

| Problem found | Possible cause | Handling suggestion |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Wavy wall | Putty spread uneven, sloppy sanding. | Phase 1 found, must rework sanding. Phase 2 found, local can sand then patch, serious need redo. |
| Serious powdering | Poor putty quality, painted before dry, over-diluted. | Need find cause. If only topcoat issue can sand then repaint; if putty issue may need local scrape redo. |
| Wall cracking | Base treatment inadequate (no mesh), uneven material shrinkage. | Mark crack, fill with joint compound, paste seam tape, re-putty paint. |
| Paint sag | Brush dipped too much paint, coated too thick. | Sand flat, then thin repaint patch. |
| Uneven color/show-through | Over-diluted, poor coverage; insufficient brush coats. | Re-brush enough coats of topcoat. |

---

## 5. Acceptance Checklist for Owners

**Phase 1 (after putty) checklist:**
- [ ] Night side light check, wall no waves, smooth light-shadow.
- [ ] 2-meter straightedge check, flatness error ≤ 4mm (key areas ≤ 3mm).
- [ ] Internal/external corners straight, 90 degrees.
- [ ] All detail corners (sockets, wall corners) handled flat clean.

**Phase 2 (after latex paint) checklist:**
- [ ] Natural light side view, paint color uniform, no sags, no particles.
- [ ] Dark cloth friction test, no obvious powdering.
- [ ] Whole house no visible cracks.
- [ ] Baseboard, door/window frame edges paint line neat, no pollution.
- [ ] Wall bottom height enough, fully covered by baseboard.
