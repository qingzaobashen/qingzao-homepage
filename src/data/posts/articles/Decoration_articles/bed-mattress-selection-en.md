# Bed and Mattress Selection Guide

## Bed Frame Selection Tips

### Flush-edge bed vs. recessed-edge bed
- The flush-edge bed is more practical: the mattress is less likely to slide, it saves space, and it is safer for daily use.
- Avoid recessed-edge beds: they make changing sheets troublesome, you can easily bump the edges, and they feel cramped in small rooms.

### Regular bed vs. storage bed
- A regular bed is safer: stable structure, comfortable feel, and good ventilation under the bed.
- Be cautious with storage beds: the bottom is poorly ventilated, the mattress easily gets damp, the storage space is not very convenient in practice, and it tends to collect dust.

### Material choice
- Prioritize solid wood frames: sturdy and durable, good eco-friendliness, no formaldehyde concerns.
- Avoid medium-density fiberboard (MDF): not durable, easily deforms over time, and carries a higher formaldehyde emission risk.

### Slat structure
- A slatted base is more reliable: sturdy and durable, good elastic support, and friendly to the mattress.
- Avoid arched frames: they deform and creak over time, and too-wide gaps may damage the mattress.

### Height and details
- A bed foot height of around 20 cm is recommended: easy to clean underneath, and a robot vacuum can get in.
- Be careful with low-foot beds: dust collects underneath and cleaning is hard.

### Headboard fabric and filling
- Top-grain leather is the best choice: good breathability, and it does not easily break down over time.
- Be cautious with faux and split leather: not breathable, stuffy in summer, and starts peeling before long.
- Down filling is more comfortable: good wrapping feel, less likely to collapse.
- Avoid plush (doll cotton) filling: it gets compressed after a while and support keeps declining.
- Headboard specs: a filling sponge density of 25–35D gives just-right support; a height of about 45 cm better supports the back and neck.

## Mattress Selection Key Points

### Latex layer
- Natural latex is the best choice: over 90% content, with 3–5 cm thickness being ideal.
- Avoid synthetic latex: worse in both performance and eco-friendliness.

### Spring structure
- Individually pocketed coils are recommended: good silent effect, no disturbance to your partner when turning, and conforms to body curves.
- Be cautious with connected (Bonnell) springs: prone to noise and disturbance.

### Purchase protection
- For online purchases, look at the trial-sleep period: choose ones with a 30+ day trial-sleep service so you can return if unsuitable.
- Be cautious with no trial-sleep guarantee: buying without trying makes returns and exchanges troublesome.

### Breathable design
- Better to have side ventilation holes: enhances internal air circulation, moisture-proofing and heat dissipation.
- Be careful with fully enclosed ones: heat and moisture cannot escape, affecting sleep hygiene.

### Material transparency
- More reassuring to have a viewing window or zipper: you can directly see the internal materials and check them against the claims.
- Be wary of fully enclosed ones: materials are invisible, possibly hiding flaws.

### Zoning design
- Three-zone is enough: head, waist, and legs each supported, targeted.
- No need for too many zones: seven-zone, nine-zone sound complicated but may actually feel less conforming.

### Fabric choice
- Quality materials like Tencel are more comfortable: skin-friendly, breathable, good moisture absorption.
- Avoid synthetic fabrics: not breathable, stuffy in summer, and prone to static.

### Price reference
- The 2000–6000 RMB range offers good value: at this price you get decent materials and craftsmanship.
- Be cautious with both very expensive and very cheap: too expensive may be brand premium, too cheap may have material issues.

## Mattress Brand References

- Like a soft bed and care about silence: check Kingkoil, e.g. the deep-pit Soft series.
- Pursue high cost-performance: Xilinmen (Sleemon) is good, with a firmer support feel.
- Want full conforming zero-pressure feel: consider Mlily (Manli), mainly memory foam, but it needs some adaptation.
- Classic spring bed: Simmons is an old brand.
- Interested in smart functions: Serta focuses on smart beds.
- Other notes: Sealy may have average silence; DeRUCCI (Muse) has no particularly outstanding feature; European/American brands are generally softer than domestic ones.
