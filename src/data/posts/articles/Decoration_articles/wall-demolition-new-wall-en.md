# Wall Demolition and New Wall Construction

---

## 1. Demolition Phase: Safety First, Full Protection

### 1.1 Preparation Before Demolition
- **Who is responsible for demolition**: Confirm the responsible party in advance. Options include the property management, the renovation company, or hiring on your own — compare prices and choose. Property management and renovation companies are usually more expensive. If you arrange it yourself, walk around a newly handed-over neighborhood to find many demolition ads, or ask neighbors and friends.
- **Pipe protection**: Use cloth or plugs to block the drainage pipes and prevent debris from falling in. Check that they are clear during acceptance.

### 1.2 Demolition Work: Three Scrapes and One Repair
- **Positioning and cutting**: First mark the cutting line on the wall with a chalk line or laser, cut both sides, then knock along the line to ensure a clean cut that does not affect surrounding walls.
- **Scrape off the whitewash layer**: Scrape off the white coating (whitewash layer) on the wall to expose the cement layer, so that subsequent tiling or painting will adhere firmly. After scraping, rinse the wall with water.
- **Scrape off the insulation layer**: Thoroughly scrape off the insulation layer on all exterior walls, including the mortar at the base. Level the exposed rebar. If you install floor heating, it is recommended **not to scrape the floor insulation layer** — better insulation, but you lose some ceiling height.
- **Repair work**: If demolition damages surrounding walls or floors, repair them level promptly before moving to the next step.
- **About hollow spots**: Tap the wall with a small hammer. If you hear a hollow sound (hollow spot), any area larger than 5 cm² must be fixed by the developer.

## 2. New Wall Construction: Standard Process

### 2.1 Before Construction: Handover and Material Inspection
- **On-site handover**: Designer, project manager, and bricklayer review the drawings together to confirm the position and size of the new wall.
- **Material acceptance**:
  - **Red brick**: Must be intact with no cracks. Pick like choosing apples — reject any with cracks.
  - **Sand**: Fine and free of impurities. Grab a handful, squeeze, and release — it should disperse naturally.
  - **Cement**: Production date within 3 months, no clumping. Choose grade 32.5R or 42.5R (higher number = higher strength).

### 2.2 Bricklaying: Five Steps Done Right
- **Positioning and pre-treatment**: Use a laser level to position the wall and ensure it is vertical. Soak red bricks in water one day in advance to prevent them from drawing moisture from the mortar.
- **Base connection**:
  1. At the joints of old and new walls, drive rebar (bar reinforcement) every 50–60 cm. Rebar diameter at least 1 cm, inserted into the old wall more than 10 cm, so the new wall will not collapse.
  2. For kitchen and bathroom, first build a waterproof beam (20–30 cm high) matching the sump pit height — like a "fence" for the kitchen and bathroom.
  3. Above door openings, pour a lintel; the distance inserted into each side wall should be no less than 20 cm (exact amount depends on site conditions).
- **Laying and finishing**: Lay bricks like building blocks, staggered (the "工" pattern). Fill the mortar in the joints. At the top of the wall, lay bricks at a slant (60° "centipede angle") to prevent cracking when the wall settles.
- **Meshing and curing**: At the joints of old and new walls, hang steel mesh on both sides (to prevent later wall cracking). After meshing, apply plaster, then sprinkle water to cure for 3 consecutive days so the cement hardens slowly and avoids cracking.
- **Level and vertical**: Use a straightedge to check the wall; gaps no more than 5 mm. Use an infrared meter to check verticality; deviation no more than 5 mm.
- **Brick combination**: Use red brick at the bottom (at least three courses), light brick or perforated brick above — sturdy and space-saving.
- **Laying height**: Perforated brick at most 1.5 m per day; light brick should be laid over 2 days to let the cement set.
- **Wrapping the flue**: Wrap the flue with red brick for the most stable result, but it takes space. If space is tight, use M-shaped steel mesh, but do not drive nails into the flue.

## 3. Acceptance Standards: Five-Step Check

1. **Check verticality**: Use a laser level and straightedge. The wall must be vertical; internal and external corners must form a 90° right angle.
2. **Check brick joints**: Shine a flashlight on the joints to see if light leaks through (mortar not filled). Plaster should be even and smooth.
3. **Check lintels**: The lintel (load-bearing beam) above doors and windows must be thick enough, inserted far enough into the wall, and the surface must be flat.
4. **Check the waterproof beam**: The kitchen/bathroom waterproof beam must be high enough (20–30 cm), continuous without breaks, matching the sunken floor section.
5. **Check pipes**: Verify drainage is clear and demolition areas are fully repaired.

---

## Summary: Key Points of Wall Renovation

1. **Demolish slowly**: Position precisely like cutting a cake, protect the pipes, and scrape the old layers clean.
2. **Build walls steadily**: Lay bricks staggered like building blocks, do the base connection well, and slant the top course to prevent cracks.
3. **Accept strictly**: Use tools to check verticality, brick joints, lintels, waterproof beams, and pipes — ensure every step passes.
4. **Mind the details**: Reasonable brick combination, controlled laying height, and correct flue-wrapping method.
